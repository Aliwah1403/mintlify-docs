## 1. Financial Overview (Bar Chart)

This chart compares your income and expenses side-by-side over time.

<img
  src="/images/financial-overview-bar-chart.png"
  alt="Financial Overview Chart"
/>

### Reading the Chart

**Axes:**

- **X-axis (Horizontal):** Time periods (dates)
- **Y-axis (Vertical):** Amount in KES

**Bars:**

- **Dark Green bars:** Total Income for that period
- **Lime Green bars:** Total Expenses for that period

**What to Look For:**

### Healthy Pattern

```
Dark green bars consistently taller than lime green bars = Profitable operation ‚úÖ
```

<img src="/images/healthy-financial-pattern.png" alt="Healthy Pattern" />

### Warning Pattern

```
Lime green bars approaching or matching dark green bars = Thin margins ‚ö†Ô∏è
```

<img src="/images/warning-financial-pattern.png" alt="Warning Pattern" />

### Crisis Pattern

```
Lime green bars taller than dark green bars = Operating at loss üö®
```

<img src="/images/crisis-financial-pattern.png" alt="Crisis Pattern" />

### Trend Analysis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion
    title="Gap widening (dark bars pulling away)"
    icon="arrows-left-right-to-line"
  >
    **Meaning:** Profit margins improving **Causes:** - Better rate negotiations
    - Fuel cost reductions - More efficient operations - Fewer repair incidents
    **Action:** Document what's working and replicate it
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gap narrowing (lime catching dark)" icon="compress">
  **Meaning:** Profit margins shrinking **Causes:** - Rising fuel costs -
  Increased repairs - Lower rates accepted - More expensive routes **Action:**
  Review expense breakdown and adjust operations
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Both bars declining" icon="arrow-trend-down">
  **Meaning:** Lower business volume **Causes:** - Seasonal slowdown - Lost
  clients - Fewer available trips - Market conditions **Action:** Focus on
  maintaining margins, consider marketing
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Both bars growing" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    **Meaning:** Business expansion **Causes:** - More clients - Additional
    vehicles - New routes - Increased demand **Action:** Ensure growth is
    profitable (check gap between bars)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### How to Use This Data

**Interactive Drill-Down:**

Click on any destination bar to see detailed trip information:

<img src="/images/destination-drill-down.png" alt="Destination Trip Details" />

**What you'll see:**

- **Registration** - Which vehicle made the trips
- **Driver** - Who drove each trip
- **Date** - When trips occurred
- **Income** - Revenue per trip
- **Expenses** - Costs per trip
- **Revenue** - Profit per trip (shown in green)

The popup shows the **5 most recent trips** to that destination, allowing you to:

- Verify trip profitability
- See which vehicles service that route
- Check driver assignments
- Review recent trip patterns

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Focus Resources">

    - If ARTCAFFE KISUMU is your #1 revenue source - Assign your most reliable

  </Tab>

<Tab title="Negotiate Rates">
  **Action:** Use volume data in rate discussions **Example:** - "We've
  completed 20 trips to your location this month" - "Our consistent service
  justifies a volume discount/rate increase"
</Tab>

<Tab title="Identify Opportunities">
  **Action:** Spot underserved high-value routes **Example:** - If JAVA CBD is

  <Tab title="Risk Management">
    **Action:** Diversify if one destination dominates **Example:** - If one

  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## 3. Expense Breakdown (Pie Chart)

Visualizes where your money is being spent by category.

<img
  src="/images/expense-breakdown-pie-chart.png"
  alt="Expense Breakdown Pie Chart"
/>

### Reading the Chart

**Segments:**

- **Fuel** (usually largest)
- **Mileage Fee**
- **Repair Cost**
- **Extra Cost**
- **Parking**

**Each segment shows:**

- Category name
- Percentage of total
- Actual amount

**Example:**

```
Fuel:        65% (KES 146,250)
Mileage:     15% (KES 33,750)
Repairs:     12% (KES 27,000)
Extra:        5% (KES 11,250)
Parking:      3% (KES 6,750)
```

### Typical Expense Ratios

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Healthy Distribution">
    **Pattern:**
    - Fuel: 50-65%
    - Mileage: 10-20%
    - Repairs: 5-15%
    - Other: 10-20%
    
    **Meaning:** Normal operating expenses ‚úÖ
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="High Fuel Costs">
    **Pattern:**
    - Fuel: 70-85%
    - Everything else combined: 15-30%
    
    **Warning Signs:**
    - Inefficient routes
    - Old vehicles with poor fuel economy
    - Not using fuel discounts
    
    **Actions:**
    - Apply fuel discounts from partner stations
    - Review route efficiency
    - Consider vehicle maintenance
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="High Repair Costs">
    **Pattern:**
    - Repairs: 25%+
    - Fuel: < 50%
    
    **Warning Signs:**
    - Aging fleet
    - Deferred maintenance catching up
    - Specific vehicle issues
    
    **Actions:**
    - Check Vehicle Performance table
    - Identify vehicles with highest repair costs
    - Consider retiring or replacing problem vehicles
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Balanced Low Costs">
    **Pattern:**
    - All categories under 20%
    - No clear largest expense
    
    **Possible Meanings:**
    - Very efficient operations ‚úÖ
    - Or incomplete expense recording ‚ö†Ô∏è
    
    **Verify:** Are all expenses being captured?
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Using Expense Data

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Reducing Fuel Costs" icon="gas-pump">
    **Strategies:** 1. Apply fuel discounts from partner stations ([Learn
    how](/features/expenses/fuel-discounts)) 2. Optimize routes to reduce
    distance 3. Maintain vehicles for better fuel efficiency 4. Train drivers on
    fuel-efficient driving 5. Track fuel usage per vehicle to spot anomalies
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Managing Repair Costs" icon="wrench">
  **Strategies:** 1. Implement preventive maintenance schedule 2. Track repair
  costs per vehicle 3. Identify chronic problem vehicles 4. Budget for expected
  repairs 5. Consider replacement vs. repair for old vehicles
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Controlling Extra Costs" icon="receipt">
    **Review regularly:** - Car washes: Can you negotiate monthly rates? -
    Traffic fines: Are drivers following rules? - Toll fees: Can routes avoid
    unnecessary tolls? - Emergency expenses: Can you prevent with better
    planning?
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## 4. Trip Completion Trend (Line Chart)

Shows daily operational activity across your fleet.

<img
  src="/images/trip-completion-trend-chart.png"
  alt="Trip Completion Trend"
/>

### Reading the Chart

**Lines:**

- Each line represents a vehicle type
- **Green line:** Trucks
- **Dark green line:** Canters
- **Orange line:** Dump Trucks
- **Blue line:** Reefer Trucks
- _(etc. for other types)_

**Axes:**

- **X-axis:** Dates
- **Y-axis:** Number of trips

### Patterns to Recognize

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Consistent Activity">
    **Pattern:** Relatively flat lines with minor variations **Meaning:** Stable
    operations **Example:** ``` Mon: 5 trips Tue: 6 trips Wed: 5 trips Thu: 7
    trips Fri: 5 trips ``` **Good for:** Predictable revenue, easier planning
  </Tab>

<Tab title="Weekly Patterns">
  **Pattern:** Peaks on certain days, valleys on others **Common:** - High
  Mon-Fri - Low Sat-Sun **Or:** - Low Mon (planning) - Peak Tue-Thu - Drop Fri
  (end of week) **Use this to:** - Schedule maintenance on low days - Plan
  driver schedules - Anticipate cash flow
</Tab>

<Tab title="Growth Trend">
  **Pattern:** Lines trending upward over time **Meaning:** Increasing business
  volume **Monitor:** - Is profit growing proportionally? - Do you need more
  vehicles? - Are drivers managing workload?
</Tab>

  <Tab title="Decline Trend">
    **Pattern:** Lines trending downward **Possible causes:** - Seasonal
    slowdown - Lost clients - Competition - Market conditions **Actions:** -
    Compare to same period last year - Review client relationships - Adjust
    fleet size if sustained
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Trend Indicators

At the bottom of this chart, you'll see a trend statement:

**Example 1:**

```
"Trending up by 12.5% üìà"
```

**Meaning:** Trip volume increased 12.5% compared to previous period

**Example 2:**

```
"Trending down by 8.3% üìâ"
```

**Meaning:** Trip volume decreased 8.3% compared to previous period

<Info>
  This percentage compares the most recent day to the previous day in your
  selected date range.
</Info>

## Using All Charts Together

### Monthly Business Review Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Financial Overview First">
    Check overall profitability - Are you making money? - Are margins healthy?
  </Step>

<Step title="Trip Volume Second">
  Review Trip Completion Trend - Is activity consistent? - Any concerning
  patterns?
</Step>

<Step title="Expense Analysis Third">
  Study Expense Breakdown - Where is money going? - Any categories too high?
</Step>

<Step title="Revenue Focus Fourth">
  Examine Top Destinations - Which routes are most valuable? - Should you focus
  resources differently?
</Step>

  <Step title="Action Items Fifth">
    Based on insights from all four charts: - Adjust operations - Update pricing
    - Reallocate vehicles - Address cost issues
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Cross-Chart Analysis Examples

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High activity + Low profit" icon="chart-mixed">
    **Scenario:** - Trip Completion Trend: High, stable - Financial Overview:
    Small gap between income/expense **Diagnosis:** You're busy but not
    profitable **Solution:** - Check Expense Breakdown for cost drivers - Review
    Top Destinations - are low-margin routes dominating? - Consider raising
    rates or reducing costs
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Low activity + High expenses" icon="triangle-exclamation">
  **Scenario:** - Trip Completion Trend: Declining - Expense Breakdown: High
  repair costs **Diagnosis:** Fewer trips + expensive maintenance = trouble
  **Solution:** - Check Vehicle Performance for problem vehicles - Consider
  fleet right-sizing - Focus marketing on high-value routes
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Growing revenue + Flat trips" icon="chart-line-up">
    **Scenario:** - Financial Overview: Income increasing - Trip Completion
    Trend: Stable/flat **Diagnosis:** Higher rates or better clients (not just
    more volume) **Analysis:** - Check Top Destinations for high-value routes -
    This is efficient growth - maintain quality over quantity
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Interactive Dashboard Features

Travada's dashboard includes several interactive elements to help you drill down into your data:

### Click-Through Analysis

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Top Destinations ‚Üí Trip Details" icon="map-location-dot">
    **How to use:** 1. Click any bar in the Top Destinations chart 2. A popup
    shows the 5 most recent trips to that destination 3. Review vehicle, driver,
    date, income, expenses, and revenue per trip **Use cases:** - Verify which
    vehicles service your best clients - Check if trips to that destination are
    consistently profitable - Identify patterns in driver assignments
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vehicle Performance ‚Üí Trip History" icon="truck">
  **How to use:** 1. Click any vehicle in the Vehicle Performance section 2. See
  the 5 most recent trips for that specific vehicle 3. Review profitability
  trends for that vehicle **Use cases:** - Investigate why a vehicle is
  unprofitable - Verify recent activity for idle vehicles - Check if a vehicle
  needs maintenance based on expenses
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Dashboard Customization" icon="sliders">
    **How to use:** 1. Click the **Edit** button in the bottom right 2. Toggle
    charts on/off in the customization dialog 3. Click **Apply** to save your
    layout **Available toggles:** - Top Destinations (can hide) - Expense
    Analysis (can hide) **Always visible:** - Financial Summary - Financial
    Overview - Trip Completion Trend - Vehicle Performance
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
