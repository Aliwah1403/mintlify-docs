## Overview

The Dashboard is your command center in Travada. It provides an at-a-glance view of your business performance, showing income, expenses, profitability, and operational metrics in one consolidated view.

![Travada Dashboard](/Screenshot2026-01-30at9.26.26am.png)

## When to Use the Dashboard

Visit your dashboard to:

- Check daily financial performance
- Monitor overall profitability
- Identify top-performing routes and vehicles
- Spot trends in trip completion
- Review expense breakdowns
- Get a quick business health check

<Tip>
  **Best Practice:** Start each day by reviewing your dashboard. It takes less than 2 minutes and gives you complete situational awareness of your operations.
</Tip>

## Dashboard Layout

The dashboard is organized into several sections, each providing specific insights:

### 1. Financial Summary Cards (Top Section)

Three key metrics displayed prominently:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card icon="coins" title="Total Income">
    Revenue from all completed trips (Rate + Detention fees)
  </Card>
  <Card icon="receipt" title="Total Expense">
    All costs incurred across your fleet
  </Card>
  <Card icon="chart-line" title="Total Revenue">
    Your profit/loss (Income - Expenses)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

![Financial Summary Cards](/images/financial-summary-cards.png)

Each card shows:

- **Current Value** - The metric for your selected time period
- **Comparison** - Growth or decline vs. previous period
- **Trend Indicator** - ↑ (increase) or ↓ (decrease)

<Info>
  **Example:** If Total Income shows "KES 12,700.00" with "↑ 15%", this means income increased by 15% compared to the previous period.
</Info>

### 2. Financial Overview Chart

A bar chart comparing income vs expenses over time.

![Financial Overview Bar Chart](/images/financial-overview-chart.png)

**What it shows:**

- Dark Green bars = Income
- Lime Green bars = Expenses
- X-axis = Time periods (days, weeks, or months depending on date filter)
- Y-axis = Amount(in your preferred currency)

**How to read it:**

- Taller dark green bars = Higher income periods
- Gap between dark and lime = Your profit margin
- Lime green bars taller than dark green = Loss period (expenses exceeded income)

<Tip>
  Look for patterns: Are certain days of the week more profitable? Are expenses rising faster than income?
</Tip>

### 3. Top Destinations by Revenue

A bar chart showing which destinations generate the most income.

![Top Destinations by Revenue](/images/top-destinations-chart.png)

**Displays:**

- Top 5 highest revenue destinations
- Horizontal bars showing relative income amounts
- Actual revenue figures

**Interactive Features:**

<Note>
  **Click any destination bar** to see the 5 most recent trips to that destination, including: - Vehicle registration - Driver name - Trip date - Income earned - Expenses incurred - Net revenue
</Note>

![Recent Trips to Destination](/images/destination-trips-popup.png)

**Use this to:**

- Focus on high-value routes
- Review trip details for specific destinations
- Negotiate better rates for top destinations
- Allocate vehicles strategically
- Identify which clients generate most business

<Info>
  **Example:** If "ARTCAFFE KISUMU" shows KES 98,400, this destination has generated the most revenue in your selected time period.
</Info>

### 4. Expense Breakdown (Pie Chart)

Visual breakdown of where your money is being spent.

![Expense Breakdown Pie Chart](/images/expense-breakdown-chart.png)

**Categories shown:**

- **Fuel** - Usually the largest expense
- **Mileage Fee** - Driver allowances
- **Repair Cost** - Vehicle maintenance
- **Extra Cost** - Miscellaneous expenses
- **Parking** - Parking fees

**Each segment shows:**

- Category name
- Percentage of total expenses
- Actual amount in KES

<Warning>
  If fuel costs exceed 60-70% of total expenses, you may have opportunities to optimize fuel efficiency or negotiate better rates with fuel stations.
</Warning>

### 5. Trip Completion Trend

A line chart showing daily trip volume by vehicle type.

![Trip Completion Trend Line Chart](/images/trip-completion-trend.png)

**What it tracks:**

- Number of trips completed per day
- Separate lines for each vehicle type (Truck, Canter, etc.)
- Trends over time

**Colors:**

- Green line = Trucks
- Dark green line = Canters
- (Additional colors for other vehicle types)

**Use this to:**

- Monitor operational activity levels
- Identify busy vs slow periods
- Plan vehicle maintenance during low-activity days
- Track seasonal patterns

### 6. Vehicle Performance

A table showing top-performing and underperforming vehicles.

![Vehicle Performance Table](/images/vehicle-performance-table.png)

**Columns:**

- Vehicle registration number
- Number of trips completed
- Total income generated
- Total expenses incurred
- Net profit (or loss)

**Sections:**

- **Top Profitable Vehicles** - Your money-makers
- **Unprofitable Vehicles** - Vehicles losing money
- **Vehicles with No Trips** - Idle vehicles

**Filter Options:**

Use the dropdown in the top right to filter by:

- All Vehicles (default)
- Specific vehicle types (Truck, Canter, Dump Truck, etc.)

![Vehicle Filter Dropdown](/images/vehicle-filter-dropdown.png)

**Interactive Features:**

<Note>
  **Click any vehicle** in the performance chart to see the 5 most recent trips for that vehicle, showing detailed trip information and profitability.
</Note>

<Tip>
  **Action Item:** If a vehicle consistently shows in the "Unprofitable" section, investigate why. It might need maintenance, have route inefficiencies, or indicate driver issues.
</Tip>

## Date Filtering

Control what data you see using the date filter in the top right:

![Date Filter Options](/images/date-filter-buttons.png)

**Quick filters:**

- **7D** - Last 7 days
- **1M** - Last month (default)
- **3M** - Last 3 months
- **6M** - Last 6 months
- **Custom** - Select specific date range

**How it works:**

- Click any button to instantly update all dashboard metrics
- All charts and cards refresh based on your selection
- Your selection persists across sessions

<Info>
  **Pro Tip:** Use 7D for daily operations monitoring, 1M for month-end reviews, and 3M/6M for strategic planning.
</Info>

## Understanding "N/A" Values

You might see "N/A" (Not Available) in some dashboard cards:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="receipt" title="Total Expense shows N/A">
    **Cause:** No expense data recorded for the selected period **Means:** - No trips with expenses have been recorded - Or all recorded trips have zero expenses **Action:** Record trips with at least one expense field filled (Fuel, Parking, etc.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="chart-simple" title="Charts appear empty">
    **Cause:** No data for the selected time period **Solutions:** - Extend your date range - Verify trips have been recorded - Check if trips are from a different time period
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Dashboard Customization

You can customize which charts appear on your dashboard using the **Edit** button.

![Dashboard Edit Button](/images/dashboard-edit-button.png)

### How to Customize Charts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Edit Button">
    Located in the bottom right corner after the Financial Overview Chart
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Charts to Display">
    A "Customise charts view" dialog will open showing available charts: **Top
    Destinations** - Top destinations by revenue over selected period - ✓ Checked = Visible - ☐ Unchecked = Hidden **Expense Analysis** - Expense patterns over selected period - Includes the Expense Breakdown pie chart - ✓ Checked = Visible - ☐ Unchecked = Hidden

    ![Customize Charts Dialog](/images/customize-charts-dialog.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview Your Selection">
    Both charts are shown with live data in the dialog so you can see what you're choosing
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply Changes">
    Click **Apply** to save your dashboard layout Or click **Cancel** to keep current settings
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Use Case:** If you primarily focus on vehicle performance and financial overview, you can hide Top Destinations and Expense Analysis to reduce dashboard clutter and focus on what matters most to you.
</Tip>

### Default Charts (Always Visible)

These charts cannot be hidden and always appear on the dashboard:

- Financial Summary Cards (Income, Expense, Profit)
- Financial Overview bar chart
- Most Recent Trips table

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="coins" title="Why don't Income and Revenue match?">
    **Answer:** They measure different things! - **Total Income** = All revenue from trips (Rate + Detention) - **Total Revenue** = Profit after expenses (Income - Expenses) Revenue will always be less than Income (unless you have no expenses). **Example:** - Income: KES 100,000 - Expenses: KES 60,000 - Revenue (Profit): KES 40,000
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="download" title="Can I export dashboard data?">
    **Current Status:** Direct dashboard export is not available **Alternative:** Generate detailed reports from the Reports section, which can be shared and contain all the underlying data. See [Generating
    Reports](/features/reports/generating-rate-report) for details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="arrows-rotate" title="How often does the dashboard update?">
    **Answer:** Real-time The dashboard updates immediately when you: - Record a new trip - Edit existing data - Apply fuel discounts - Change date filters No need to refresh the page manually.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion icon="arrow-trend-down" title="What if I see negative revenue?">
    **Meaning:** Your expenses exceeded your income for that period **Common
    causes:** - High repair costs - Low trip volume - Expensive fuel period - One-time major expense **Action:** - Review expense breakdown to identify the main cost driver - Check vehicle performance for underperforming vehicles - Investigate if rates need adjustment
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quick Wins from Dashboard Insights

Use your dashboard data to make quick improvements:

### If fuel costs are too high:

1. Check the Expense Breakdown pie chart
2. Go to Expenses → Fuel to see which vehicles use most fuel
3. Apply fuel discounts from partner stations
4. Consider route optimization for high-fuel vehicles

### If certain vehicles are unprofitable:

1. Check Vehicle Performance table
2. Identify consistently unprofitable vehicles
3. Review their trip history in Expenditure
4. Consider reassigning to different routes or scheduling maintenance

### If certain destinations are highly profitable:

1. Check Top Destinations chart
2. Identify your top 2-3 revenue generators
3. Focus marketing efforts on those clients/routes
4. Allocate your best vehicles to those destinations
