## Overview

The **Detention** tab shows additional income from container delay charges. These fees compensate you when your trucks are delayed at ports or depots due to unpaid logistics charges or clearance issues.

<img src='/images/income-detention-tab.png' alt='Detention Tab' />

## What are Detention Fees?

**Detention fees** are charges applied when trucks carrying containers are delayed due to:

- ❌ Unpaid import duties
- ❌ Missing documentation
- ❌ Clearance holds
- ❌ Client payment delays
- ❌ Port authority issues

**Not driver's fault** - these delays are caused by external factors beyond the driver's control.

<Info>
  Detention fees compensate you for: - Lost productivity (truck sitting idle) -
  Driver waiting time - Opportunity cost (could have done other trips) -
  Additional operational costs
</Info>

## When Do Detention Fees Apply?

<Tabs>
  <Tab title='Container Operations'>
    **Primary scenario:** - Trailer trucks at ports/depots - Picking up or
    delivering containers - Cargo clearance required **Typical locations:** -
    Mombasa Port - ICD Nairobi (Inland Container Depot) - KPA depots - Private
    container yards
  </Tab>

<Tab title='Delay Causes'>
  **Common reasons for detention:** ✅ **Client-side:** - Unpaid import duties -
  Missing paperwork - Payment not cleared - Authorization delays ✅
  **Authority-side:** - Customs inspections - Cargo holds - Documentation
  verification - Security checks ✅ **Logistics:** - Clerk fee unpaid -
  Container not released - System errors
</Tab>

  <Tab title='Who Pays'>
    **Detention charged to:** - The party responsible for delay - Usually the
    client/importer - Sometimes shared if dispute **You bill for:** - Time truck
    was held - Agreed detention rate - Per day or per hour
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Typical Detention Rates

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='Container Size' icon='box'>
    **20ft Container:** - KES 5,000 - 15,000 per day - Smaller, less impact
    **40ft Container:** - KES 10,000 - 30,000 per day - Larger, more valuable
    cargo - Higher detention rates
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title='Time Periods' icon='clock'>
  **Hourly rates:** - First 2 hours: Often free (grace period) - After 2 hours:
  KES 1,000 - 2,000 per hour **Daily rates:** - Day 1: KES 10,000 - 15,000 - Day
  2+: KES 15,000 - 25,000 (escalating) **Weekly:** - Can accumulate to KES
  100,000+ for prolonged delays
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title='Client Agreements' icon='file-contract'>
    **Typical contract terms:** - Grace period: 2-4 hours free - Standard rate:
    KES 15,000 per day - Escalation: +KES 5,000 after day 3 - Maximum cap: KES
    150,000 **Important:** Document detention terms in client contracts upfront
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What's Displayed

The Detention tab shows:

| Column            | Description               |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Registration**  | Vehicle that was detained |
| **Driver**        | Who was waiting           |
| **Destination**   | Port/depot location       |
| **Trip Date**     | When detention occurred   |
| **Detention Fee** | Amount charged (KES)      |

**Total Detention:** Sum displayed at top right

<Note>
  Rate fees are **not** shown in this tab - only detention charges. See Rate tab
  for base trip income.
</Note>

## Common Detention Scenarios

<Tabs>
  <Tab title='Standard Port Delay'>
    **Situation:** - Trailer to Mombasa Port - Container ready but client hasn't
    paid duties - Truck waits 1 day **Charges:** - Rate fee: KES 125,000
    (transport) - Detention: KES 15,000 (1 day wait) - Total: KES 140,000
    **Documentation:** - Gate entry timestamp - Release approval time -
    Communication with client **Invoice:** Itemize both charges separately
  </Tab>

<Tab title='Multi-Day Detention'>
  **Situation:** - Import clearance delayed 3 days - Escalating detention rates
  **Charges:** - Day 1: KES 15,000 - Day 2: KES 15,000 - Day 3: KES 20,000
  (escalated) - Total detention: KES 50,000 **Entry in Travada:** - Single trip
  with KES 50,000 detention - Or multiple trip entries per day (depends on your
  process)
</Tab>

  <Tab title='Partial Detention'>
    **Situation:** - Delay of 6 hours - Hourly detention after 2-hour grace
    **Calculation:** - Grace: 2 hours (free) - Billable: 4 hours × KES 2,000 =
    KES 8,000 **Entry:** - Detention: KES 8,000 **Document:** Time logs clearly
    show delay duration
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Best Practices for Detention

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title='Document Everything' icon='camera'>
    Take photos of: - Gate entry timestamps - Truck parked and waiting - Release
    paperwork - Communication (WhatsApp, emails)
  </Card>

<Card title='Clear Contracts' icon='file-contract'>
  Detention terms should specify: - Grace period - Hourly/daily rates -
  Escalation schedule - Maximum caps
</Card>

<Card title='Immediate Communication' icon='message'>
  Notify client as soon as detention starts: - "Your container is held" -
  "Detention starting after grace period" - "Please expedite clearance"
</Card>

  <Card title='Separate from Rate' icon='split'>
    Always enter detention separately: - Rate: KES 125,000 - Detention: KES
    15,000 Not combined: Rate: KES 140,000
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Billing Detention to Clients

### Creating Detention Invoices

Currently, detention invoices are combined with rate invoices:

<Steps>
  <Step title='Go to Rate Tab'>Income → Rate tab (not Detention tab)</Step>

<Step title='Filter Trips'>Include trips with detention charges</Step>

<Step title='Generate Report'>
  The report will show both: - Rate fees - Detention fees (if applicable)
</Step>

  <Step title='Client Invoice'>
    Report itemizes: - Transport: KES 125,000 - Detention: KES 15,000 -
    Subtotal: KES 140,000 - VAT (16%): KES 22,400 - Total: KES 162,400
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Even though you view detention in its own tab, invoicing happens through the
  Rate report generation feature.
</Tip>

## Avoiding Detention Disputes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='Pre-Trip Confirmation' icon='clipboard-check'>

    Check documentation is complete **Prevents:** Unnecessary detention
  </Accordion>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='When can I charge detention?' icon='question'>
    **You can charge when:** - Delay is NOT driver's fault - Delay is due to
    client/authority issues - You have proof of waiting time - It's in your
    contract **You cannot charge when:** - Driver was late - Truck breakdown -
    Your operational issues
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title='How long is grace period?' icon='clock'>
  **Typical:** 2-4 hours **Varies by:** - Client agreement - Industry standard -
  Type of operation **Common:** 2 hours for ports, 4 hours for depots
</Accordion>

<Accordion title='What if client refuses to pay?' icon='hand'>
  **Steps:** 1. Show documented proof 2. Reference contract terms 3. Negotiate
  if needed 4. Escalate if unresolved **Prevention:** Clear contracts upfront
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title='Can detention exceed rate fee?' icon='greater-than'>
    **Answer:** Yes, it can! **Example:** - Rate: KES 125,000 (transport) -
    Detention: KES 150,000 (5 days @ KES 30,000) - Total: KES 275,000 **Why?**
    Prolonged delays are very costly
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Detention Analysis

Use the Detention tab to identify:

### Patterns

- Which clients cause most detention?
- Which ports/depots have issues?
- Which drivers experience more delays?

### Optimization

- Avoid problem clients/routes
- Adjust pricing for high-detention clients
- Negotiate better terms

### Financial Impact

- Total detention income per month
- Percentage of total income
- Trend over time (increasing/decreasing)

<Info>
  For most fleets, detention is 5-15% of total income. If higher, investigate
  operational causes.
</Info>
