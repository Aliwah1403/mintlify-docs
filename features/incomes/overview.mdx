## Overview

The Income section in Travada provides a comprehensive view of all revenue earned from your trips. It automatically organizes income data by type, helps you verify earnings, and enables you to generate professional invoices for clients.

<img src="/images/income-page.png" alt="Income Page Overview" />

## What is Income in Travada?

Income represents all money earned from completed trips. Travada tracks two main types of income:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Rate Fees" icon="money-bill">
    Base trip charges - what you invoice clients for transport services
  </Card>
  <Card title="Detention Fees" icon="clock">
    Additional charges for container delays (primarily trailer trucks)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Income data flows automatically from trip recording. When you enter Rate or Detention fees in Expenditure, they appear here instantly.
</Info>

## How Income Data Flows

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trip Recorded">
    You record a trip in Expenditure with Rate and/or Detention fee
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Income Captured">
    System automatically extracts income amounts
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Categorized">
    Income sorted into Rate and Detention tabs
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Dashboard Updated">
    Total Income card on Dashboard refreshes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Available for Reports">
    Income data ready for client invoicing
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Income Page Layout

The Income section has **three tabs**:

<img src="/images/income-rate-tab.png" alt="Rate Tab" />

### 3. Detention

<img src="/images/income-detention-tab.png" alt="Detention Tab" />

- Container delay fees

**Use for:**
- Port/container operations billing
- Detention-specific invoicing
- Delay charge verification

## Understanding Income Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Rate Fees">
    **What it is:**
    - Base charge for transporting goods
    - Agreed upon before trip
    - Most consistent income source
    
    **Typical amounts:**
    - Canter: KES 3,000 - 20,000
    - Truck: KES 10,000 - 100,000
    - Trailer: KES 80,000 - 200,000
    - Dump truck: KES 5,000 - 30,000
    - Reefer: KES 50,000 - 150,000
    
    **When charged:**
    - Every trip (nearly)
    - Based on distance, cargo, agreement
    - What you invoice clients
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Detention Fees">
    **What it is:**
    - Extra charges when trucks carrying containers are delayed
    - Happens at ports/depots when clearance delayed
    - Due to unpaid logistics charges
    
    **Typical amounts:**
    - KES 5,000 - 50,000+ per day
    - Varies by container size
    - Accumulates daily
    
    **When charged:**
    - Container operations only
    - When truck waits due to client delays
    - Billable to client who caused delay
    
    **Common scenarios:**
    - Port clearance delays
    - Unpaid import duties
    - Documentation issues
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Common Workflows

### Daily Review

**End of day:**
1. Navigate to Income → All
2. Set date filter to today
3. Verify all trips recorded
4. Check total income matches expectations
5. Identify any missing entries

### Weekly Verification

**Every Monday:**
1. Go to Income → All
2. Filter last 7 days
3. Compare with trip sheets
4. Verify amounts with drivers
5. Check for discrepancies

### Client Billing

**When invoicing client:**
1. Go to Income → Rate tab
2. Filter by destination (client location)
3. Filter by date range (billing period)
4. Review trips for accuracy
5. Click "Generate Report"
6. Download/share with client

See: [Client Invoicing Guide](/guides/client-invoicing)

### Month-End

**End of month:**
1. Income → All tab
2. Filter to full month
3. Export income data
4. Verify against accounting records
5. Generate client reports
6. Archive for records

## Income vs. Expenses vs. Revenue

Understanding the relationship:

```
Income (What you earned)
  - Rate fees: KES 500,000
  - Detention fees: KES 50,000
  Total Income: KES 550,000

Expenses (What you spent)
  - Fuel: KES 200,000
  - Other costs: KES 100,000
  Total Expenses: KES 300,000

Revenue (Profit)
  Income - Expenses = Revenue
  KES 550,000 - KES 300,000 = KES 250,000
```

<Info>
  **Dashboard Note:** Dashboard shows "Total Profit" (not "Total Revenue"). Both terms mean the same: Income minus Expenses.
</Info>

<Tabs>

## Income Data Sources

All income comes from trip recording:

| Expenditure Field | Appears In |
|-------------------|------------|
| Rate | Income → Rate tab |
| Detention Fee | Income → Detention tab |
| Both | Income → All tab |

<Warning>
  **Important:** You cannot edit income directly in the Income section. To change income amounts, you must edit the original trip in Expenditure (or delete and re-record).
</Warning>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is my income total different from dashboard?" icon="calculator">
    **Possible causes:**
    1. Different date filters applied
    2. Dashboard showing all-time, Income filtered to period
    3. Recent trip not yet synced (refresh)
    
    **Solution:**
    - Ensure same date range
    - Refresh both pages
    - Check filter settings
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I add income without a trip?" icon="plus">
    **Answer:** No
    
    **Reason:** All income must be linked to trips for accountability
    
    **Workaround:** Create a trip entry even if it's an adjustment
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How do I fix wrong income amount?" icon="pen-to-square">
    **Answer:** Edit the trip in Expenditure
    
    **Steps:**
    1. Find trip in Expenditure page
    2. Note correct amount
    3. Delete trip (Admin only)
    4. Re-record with correct amount
    
    See: [Editing Trips](/features/expenditure/editing-trip)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What if I have other income sources?" icon="question">
    **Examples:** 
    - Fuel discount kickbacks
    - Insurance payouts
    - Rental income from vehicles
    
    **Travada solution:** Currently not supported
    
    **Workaround:** Track separately in accounting system
    
    **Future:** May add "Other Income" category
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
