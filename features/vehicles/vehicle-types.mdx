## Overview

Travada supports 7 vehicle types, each designed for different logistics operations. Choosing the correct type ensures accurate categorization, better reporting, and meaningful analytics.

## The 7 Vehicle Types

<Info>
  Choose the type that best matches your vehicle's **primary use**, not just its
  physical characteristics.
</Info>

## 1. Truck

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-truck.png' alt='Standard Truck' />

### Description

General-purpose transport trucks for standard cargo operations.

### Best For

- Multi-purpose hauling
- General cargo transport
- Medium to large loads
- Non-specialized operations

### Common Vehicle Models

- Isuzu FRR, FSR, FVR series
- Mitsubishi Fuso Fighter
- Hino 500 series
- UD (Nissan) trucks
- Mercedes-Benz Atego

### Typical Cargo

- Packaged goods
- Mixed cargo
- General merchandise
- Building materials (non-bulk)
- Furniture and equipment

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Short to long-haul
- **Rate Range:** KES 10,000 - 100,000 per trip
- **Fuel Efficiency:** Moderate
- **Common Destinations:** Warehouses, distribution centers, retail locations

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Default choice for standard transport operations  
✅ Vehicle doesn't fit specialized categories  
✅ Multi-purpose usage across different cargo types

❌ Don't use if:

- Vehicle has specialized equipment (use specific type)
- Primarily carries one specialized cargo type

## 2. Canter

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-canter.png' alt='Canter Truck' />

### Description

Small to medium delivery trucks, typically under 3 tons capacity. Named after the popular Mitsubishi Canter but includes all similar-sized light trucks.

### Best For

- Urban/local deliveries
- Light cargo operations
- City route services
- Last-mile delivery

### Common Vehicle Models

- Mitsubishi Canter
- Isuzu NKR, NPR, NQR
- Toyota Dyna
- Hino Dutro (300 series)
- Nissan Cabstar

### Typical Cargo

- Small packages and parcels
- Retail deliveries
- Light equipment
- Food & beverage (non-refrigerated)
- Office supplies

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Mostly local/short-haul (within 100km)
- **Rate Range:** KES 3,000 - 20,000 per trip
- **Fuel Efficiency:** High (relative to load)
- **Common Destinations:** Shops, offices, restaurants, homes

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Vehicle capacity under 3 tons  
✅ Primarily urban/city operations  
✅ Multiple daily short trips  
✅ Quick turnaround deliveries

❌ Don't use if:

- Vehicle exceeds 3-ton capacity
- Primarily long-haul operations
- Specialized cargo (refrigerated, bulk materials)

## 3. Dump Truck

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-dump-truck.png' alt='Dump Truck' />

### Description

Trucks with hydraulic tipping mechanisms for bulk material transport. Also called tippers or tipper trucks.

### Best For

- Construction material transport
- Quarry operations
- Sand, gravel, stones
- Site-to-site bulk cargo

### Common Vehicle Models

- Isuzu FVZ tipper
- Hino 700 series tipper
- UD Quester tipper
- Mercedes-Benz Actros tipper
- Shacman dump trucks

### Typical Cargo

- Sand and gravel
- Crushed stones (ballast, murram)
- Soil and dirt
- Construction rubble
- Agricultural bulk (maize, wheat in season)

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Variable (quarry to site)
- **Rate Range:** KES 5,000 - 30,000 per trip
- **Trips per Day:** Often multiple (3-10 trips common)
- **Fuel Efficiency:** Lower (heavy loads)
- **Common Destinations:** Construction sites, quarries, farms

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Vehicle has tipping/dumping mechanism  
✅ Primarily hauls bulk materials  
✅ Construction industry operations  
✅ Quarry-to-site transport

❌ Don't use if:

- Standard flatbed without tipper
- Rarely carries bulk materials
- Enclosed body truck

### Special Considerations

Dump trucks often have:

- Multiple trips per day to track separately
- Route-specific profitability (some quarries more profitable)
- Higher fuel costs per trip
- Wear-and-tear from heavy loads

## 4. Reefer Truck

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-reefer.png' alt='Reefer Truck' />

### Description

Refrigerated trucks for temperature-sensitive cargo. Essential for cold chain logistics.

### Best For

- Flower export transport
- Fresh produce distribution
- Frozen foods
- Pharmaceutical cold chain
- Temperature-controlled cargo

### Common Vehicle Models

- Isuzu with reefer unit
- Hino with reefer unit
- UD with reefer unit
- Purpose-built reefer trucks
- (Various trucks fitted with Carrier, Thermo King units)

### Typical Cargo

- Fresh flowers (roses, carnations for export)
- Fresh fruits and vegetables
- Dairy products
- Frozen meat and fish
- Pharmaceuticals and vaccines
- Ice cream and frozen foods

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Often long-haul (to airports, ports)
- **Rate Range:** KES 50,000 - 150,000+ per trip
- **Fuel Costs:** Higher (refrigeration unit)
- **Time Sensitivity:** Critical (spoilage risk)
- **Common Destinations:** JKIA cargo, Mombasa port, flower farms

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Vehicle has refrigeration unit  
✅ Primarily temperature-controlled cargo  
✅ Cold chain operations  
✅ Perishable goods transport

❌ Don't use if:

- No refrigeration equipment
- Occasionally carries cold cargo in ice boxes
- Standard insulated box without cooling

### Special Considerations

Reefer trucks have:

- Higher fuel costs (reefer unit runs constantly)
- Maintenance of cooling system
- Temperature monitoring requirements
- Premium rates for service
- Time-critical deliveries

## 5. Box Truck

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-box.png' alt='Box Truck' />

### Description

Rigid trucks with enclosed box bodies for protected cargo transport. Also called box-body or rigid trucks.

### Best For

- Dry goods transport
- Retail distribution
- Palletized cargo
- Weather-protected deliveries

### Common Vehicle Models

- Isuzu FRR/FSR box body
- Hino 500 box body
- UD box truck
- Mercedes box truck
- Custom-built box bodies on various chassis

### Typical Cargo

- Boxed goods and cartons
- Electronics and appliances
- Clothing and textiles
- Household goods
- Retail merchandise
- Office furniture

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Variable (local to long-haul)
- **Rate Range:** KES 15,000 - 80,000 per trip
- **Fuel Efficiency:** Moderate
- **Loading:** Rear door, sometimes side access
- **Common Destinations:** Retail stores, warehouses, distribution centers

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Enclosed box body (not flatbed)  
✅ Cargo needs weather protection  
✅ Palletized or boxed goods  
✅ Retail/distribution operations

❌ Don't use if:

- Open flatbed truck
- Specialized cargo (refrigerated, bulk)
- Tipper mechanism

## 6. Trailer Truck

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-trailer.png' alt='Trailer Truck' />

### Description

Articulated trucks for container transport and long-haul operations. Also called container trucks, semi-trucks, or prime movers.

### Best For

- 20ft/40ft container transport
- Port operations
- Long-distance hauls
- Import/export logistics

### Common Vehicle Models

- Isuzu Giga prime mover
- Hino 700 series
- UD Quester
- Mercedes-Benz Actros
- Shacman tractors
- Powerstar tractors

### Typical Cargo

- Shipping containers (20ft, 40ft)
- Heavy machinery
- Large equipment
- Bulk containerized goods
- Import/export cargo

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Typically long-haul
- **Rate Range:** KES 80,000 - 200,000+ per trip
- **Detention Fees:** Common (port delays)
- **Clerk Fees:** Often required
- **Common Destinations:** Mombasa port, ICD Nairobi, factories, depots

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Articulated truck with detachable trailer  
✅ Container transport operations  
✅ Port/depot operations  
✅ Long-haul interstate

❌ Don't use if:

- Rigid truck (not articulated)
- Doesn't haul containers
- Local/urban operations only

### Special Considerations

Trailer trucks have:

- Detention charges for delays
- Clerk fees for port operations
- Container tracking (number, weight, size)
- Longer trip duration
- Higher operating costs
- Complex logistics (pickup, delivery, return)

## 7. Passenger Transport Vehicle

<img src='/images/vehicle-type-passenger.png' alt='Passenger Van' />

### Description

Vans and buses for people transport rather than cargo. Different financial model from freight.

### Best For

- School transport
- Tour operations
- City shuttle services
- Employee transport
- Charter services

### Common Vehicle Models

- Toyota Hiace
- Nissan Caravan
- Mercedes-Benz Sprinter
- Ford Transit
- Volkswagen Crafter
- Buses (various)

### Typical Operations

- School runs (morning/afternoon)
- Airport transfers
- Corporate shuttles
- Tour groups
- Event transport

### Trip Characteristics

- **Distance:** Variable (routes or tours)
- **Rate Structure:** Per route, per passenger, or per day
- **Frequency:** Scheduled routes or on-demand
- **Fuel Costs:** Lower per trip vs. cargo
- **Common Destinations:** Schools, hotels, airports, tour sites

### When to Choose This Type

✅ Primarily carries people  
✅ Passenger seating configuration  
✅ School, tour, or shuttle operations  
✅ No cargo/freight operations

❌ Don't use if:

- Converted for cargo use
- Mixed cargo/passenger use (choose primary use)

### Special Considerations

Passenger vehicles have:

- Route-based profitability
- Per-passenger or per-route income
- Different expense patterns
- Regulatory requirements (PSV license)
- Fuel costs lower relative to cargo trucks
- Insurance differences

## Type Selection Quick Reference

Use this flowchart to choose:

```
What does it primarily carry?

├─ People → Passenger Transport Vehicle
│
├─ 20ft/40ft Containers → Trailer Truck
│
├─ Temperature-controlled cargo → Reefer Truck
│
├─ Bulk materials that dump/tip → Dump Truck
│
├─ Light cargo, under 3 tons, city routes → Canter
│
├─ Enclosed box for dry goods → Box Truck
│
└─ General cargo, doesn't fit above → Truck
```

## Changing Vehicle Types

If you realize you chose the wrong type:

1. Go to [Editing Vehicles](/features/vehicles/editing-vehicle)
2. Update the vehicle type
3. Future trips will use new type
4. Historical trips remain under old type

<Info>
  It's okay to change types - better late than never for accurate
  categorization!
</Info>
