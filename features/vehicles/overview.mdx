## Overview

The Vehicles section in Travada is your fleet management hub. Here you manage all vehicle information, track performance, and control which vehicles are active in the system.

<img src='/images/vehicles-page.png' alt='Vehicles Management Page' />

## Why Vehicle Management Matters

Your vehicles are the foundation of your logistics business. Proper vehicle management in Travada enables:

- ✅ **Accurate trip tracking** - Assign trips to correct vehicles
- ✅ **Performance analysis** - See which vehicles are profitable
- ✅ **Cost attribution** - Track expenses per vehicle
- ✅ **Fleet optimization** - Make data-driven decisions about your fleet
- ✅ **Maintenance planning** - Identify vehicles needing service

<Info>
  Every trip in Travada must be assigned to a vehicle. Without vehicles in your
  system, you cannot record trips.
</Info>

## Vehicle Information Tracked

Each vehicle in Travada contains:

| Field                   | Description                    | Editable |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------ | -------- |
| **Registration Number** | License plate (e.g., KBE 127H) | Yes      |
| **Vehicle Type**        | Category (Truck, Canter, etc.) | Yes      |
| **Ownership Status**    | Owned or Hired                 | Yes      |
| **Status**              | Active or Disabled             | Yes      |
| **Trip Count**          | Total trips made               | Auto     |
| **Performance Data**    | Income, expenses, profit       | Auto     |

## Ownership Status

Vehicles can be marked as:

**Owned**

- Vehicles your company owns
- Capital assets
- Long-term fleet members

**Hired**

- Leased or rented vehicles
- Temporary additions
- Contract-based

<Info>
  This distinction helps with financial reporting, asset tracking, and cost
  analysis. Hired vehicles may have different expense patterns than owned
  vehicles.
</Info>

## Vehicle Lifecycle in Travada

<Steps>
  <Step title='Add Vehicle'>
    User creates vehicle record with registration, type, and ownership See:
    [Adding Vehicles](/features/vehicles/adding-vehicle)
  </Step>

<Step title='Assign to Trips'>
  Vehicle appears in trip recording dropdowns users can assign trips to this
  vehicle
</Step>

<Step title='Track Performance'>
  System automatically calculates: - Total trips completed - Income generated -
  Expenses incurred - Net profit/loss
</Step>

<Step title='Edit as Needed'>
  Users can update vehicle type or ownership status See: [Editing
  Vehicles](/features/vehicles/editing-vehicle)
</Step>

<Step title='Disable When Inactive'>
  When vehicle is sold, contracts end, or temporarily out of service See:
  [Disabling Vehicles](/features/vehicles/disabling-vehicles)
</Step>

  <Step title='Re-enable if Needed'>
    Disabled vehicles can be reactivated later All historical data remains
    intact
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Workflows

### Daily Operations

**Normal day-to-day:**

- Vehicles remain in system
- Used for trip assignments
- Performance tracked automatically
- No vehicle management needed

<Info>
  Most days you won't need to access vehicle management - vehicles just work in
  the background!
</Info>

### Monthly Review

**End of month:**

1. Review Dashboard → Vehicle Performance
2. Identify top performers
3. Identify unprofitable vehicles
4. Check for vehicles with no trips
5. Investigate any anomalies

### Fleet Changes

**When adding new vehicles:**

1. Navigate to Vehicles → Add/Manage Vehicles
2. Click **Add Vehicle**
3. Enter registration, type, ownership
4. Vehicle immediately available for trips

**When retiring vehicles:**

1. Navigate to Vehicles → Disabled Vehicles
2. Click **+ Disable Vehicle**
3. Select vehicle(s) to disable
4. Confirm - removes from active use

## Vehicle Performance Insights

From the Dashboard, you can see:

### Top Profitable Vehicles

Vehicles that generate the most profit:

- High income generation
- Lower expense ratios
- Consistent performance

**Action:** Prioritize these vehicles for high-value routes

### Unprofitable Vehicles

Vehicles losing money:

- High repair costs
- Low trip volume
- Inefficient routes

**Action:** Investigate why and address:

- Need maintenance?
- Wrong routes?
- Should be retired?

### Vehicles with No Trips

Active but unused vehicles:

- Not assigned any trips
- Potentially forgotten
- Maybe should be disabled

**Action:** Verify status and disable if truly inactive

## Vehicle vs. Driver Management

Understanding the difference:

| Aspect               | Vehicles        | Drivers           |
| -------------------- | --------------- | ----------------- |
| **What**             | Physical assets | Human resources   |
| **Typical Count**    | 5-50            | 5-50              |
| **Change Frequency** | Low (years)     | Medium (months)   |
| **Key Metric**       | Profitability   | Performance       |
| **Ownership**        | Owned/Hired     |     -             |
| **When Inactive**    | Disable         | Disable           |

<Tip>
  Both vehicles and drivers should be disabled (not deleted) when no longer
  active to preserve historical data integrity.
</Tip>
