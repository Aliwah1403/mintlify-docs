Every report generated in Travada has a shareable link that can be sent to anyone — no login required. This makes it easy to send professional invoices to clients, share expense summaries with management, or hand over documentation to your accountant.

## How Report Sharing Works

When a report is generated, Travada creates a unique, shareable URL for that report. Anyone with the link can open and view it in a clean, formatted layout directly in their browser.

There is no need for the recipient to have a Travada account.

## How to Share a Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Reports section">
    From the main menu, click **Reports**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the report you want to share">
    Use the search bar or scroll through the All Reports list to locate it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Copy Link">
    Select **Copy link** from the Actions column next to the report. The URL is now in your clipboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Paste and send">
    Paste the link into WhatsApp, email, SMS, or any messaging platform and send it to your recipient.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Who to Share Reports With

### Clients

Use Rate Reports to share professional invoices with clients after a job is completed. The report includes container details, VAT, pickup location, and the total amount due — everything a client needs to process payment.

<Tip>
Send the report link the same day the trip is completed. Clients who receive invoices promptly tend to pay faster.
</Tip>

### Finance and Accounts Team

Share Expense Reports with your internal finance team for month-end reconciliation or bookkeeping. They can open the report directly without needing system access.

### Management

Share either report type with directors or owners who want visibility into operations without logging into Travada. A monthly summary report shared via WhatsApp is enough for most management reviews.

### Auditors and Accountants

Reports are formatted clearly enough to hand directly to an external accountant. For audit purposes, generate reports covering the full period under review and share the links or save them externally before they expire.

## Important: Links Are Not Password-Protected

<Warning>
Anyone with a report link can open it — there is no login or password required. Only share links with people who are authorized to see that financial information. Avoid posting links in group chats where unintended recipients may see them.
</Warning>

## Sharing via Different Channels

| Channel | Best For |
|---------|---------|
| WhatsApp | Clients, drivers, quick internal sharing |
| Email | Formal client invoices, accountants, auditors |
| SMS | Quick link delivery when WhatsApp is not available |
| Slack / Teams | Internal team sharing |

## Tracking Who Has Viewed a Report

The Reports section shows a **Views** count for each report. Each time someone opens the link, the count increases by one.

Use this to confirm whether a client has actually opened an invoice you sent. If the count stays at zero after a few days, follow up — they may not have received the link.

## Before a Report Expires

Reports expire after **30 days**. If you need to keep a permanent copy of a shared report:

1. Open the report before it expires
2. Print it or save it as a PDF
3. Store it in your own records

You can also regenerate the report at any time using the same date range, which creates a fresh 30-day window.

<Tip>
For clients who need long-term invoice access (e.g., for their own accounting), save a PDF copy when you generate the report and attach it to your email in addition to sharing the link.
</Tip>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I share a report with someone who does not have internet access?">
    The report link requires an internet connection to open. For recipients without reliable internet, open the report yourself, take a screenshot or save it as a PDF, and share the file directly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I resend a link after the report expires?">
    Expired report links no longer work. You will need to regenerate the report from the Expenses or Incomes section and share the new link.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A client says the link is not working">
    First check whether the report has expired in your Reports section. If it is still active, try copying the link again and resending — it may have been cut off or corrupted in the original message.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I restrict who sees a report?">
    Not currently. All report links are open to anyone who has the URL. If you need to share sensitive data selectively, consider opening the report yourself and sharing a screenshot of only the relevant section.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
