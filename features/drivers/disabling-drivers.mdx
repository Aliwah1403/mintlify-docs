## Overview

Disabling drivers is how you remove them from active use in Travada while preserving all historical trip data. This is the recommended approach when drivers leave, contracts end, or drivers go inactive.

<img src="/images/inactive-drivers-page.png" alt="Inactive Drivers Page" />

## Disable vs. Delete

Understanding the critical difference:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="✅ Disable (Recommended)">
    **What happens:** - Driver removed from trip dropdowns - All trip history
    preserved - Can be re-enabled later - Still appears in historical reports -
    Assignment data intact **Use when:** - Driver resigned - Contract ended -
    Temporarily suspended - Seasonal worker off-season - Any time driver won't
    be assigned trips
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="❌ Delete (Not Recommended)">
    **What happens:** - Driver completely removed - All trip assignments
    orphaned - Reports may show missing data - Cannot be undone **Use when:** -
    Almost never! - Only for test drivers with no trip data - Only for
    duplicates never used
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  **Always Disable, Never Delete!** Deleting drivers destroys trip assignment
  records and can corrupt your historical data.
</Warning>

## When to Disable Drivers

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Driver Resigned" icon="door-open">
    **Scenario:** Driver quit or terminated **Action:** Disable immediately
    after last day **Reason:** - No longer available for assignments -
    Historical trip data needed for accounting - May need for disputes or
    verification **Note:** Keep disabled for at least one full year
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Contract Ended" icon="file-contract">
  **Scenario:** Temporary or contract driver's term finished **Action:** Disable
  when contract expires **Reason:** - Driver no longer employed - Historical
  data needed for contract verification - Performance review for potential
  rehire **Tip:** Set calendar reminder for contract end dates
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Long-Term Leave" icon="umbrella-beach">
  **Scenario:** Driver on extended medical leave, suspension, or sabbatical
  **Action:** Disable while away (2+ weeks) **Reason:** - Prevents accidental
  assignment - Clearly shows unavailability - Can re-enable upon return
  **Duration:** Any absence over 2 weeks
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Seasonal Workers" icon="calendar">
  **Scenario:** Driver only works certain times of year **Action:** Disable
  during off-season **Reason:** - Cleaner active driver list - Focuses team on
  available drivers - Easy to re-enable when needed **Example:** School van
  drivers disabled during holidays
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Permanent Departure" icon="person-walking-arrow-right">
    **Scenario:** Driver retired or permanently left logistics industry
    **Action:** Disable permanently **Reason:** - Historical data preserved -
    Complete employment records - Audit trail maintained **Note:** Will never
    re-enable, but keep in system
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Disable a Driver

### Single Driver

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Inactive Drivers">
    Click **Drivers** → **Inactive Drivers** in sidebar
    <img
      src="/images/inactive-drivers-nav.png"
      alt="Inactive Drivers Navigation"
    />
  </Step>

<Step title="Click Disable Driver Button">
  Click **+ Disable Driver** button (top right)
  <img src="/images/disable-driver-button.png" alt="Disable Driver Button" />
</Step>

<Step title="Select Driver">
  A dialog opens with checkboxes for all active drivers Check the box next to
  the driver(s) you want to disable
  <img src="/images/disable-driver-dialog.png" alt="Disable Driver Dialog" />
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm Disable">
  Click **Disable** or **Confirm** Success message appears
</Step>

  <Step title="Verify">
    - Driver disappears from Add/Manage Drivers list - Driver appears in
    Inactive Drivers list - No longer in trip dropdown menus
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Multiple Drivers

You can disable several drivers at once:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Disable Dialog">
    Navigate to Inactive Drivers → Click **+ Disable Driver**
  </Step>

<Step title="Select Multiple">
  Check boxes for all drivers you want to disable **Use cases:** - All drivers
  from ended contracts - Entire seasonal team (e.g., all school van drivers) -
  Multiple resignations
</Step>

  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Disable** All selected drivers disabled at once
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Batch Disabling:** If multiple contract drivers leaving at once, disable
  them all together for efficiency.
</Tip>

## What Happens When You Disable

Immediate effects:

✅ **Removed from:**

- Trip recording dropdowns
- Active driver lists
- Future trip assignments

✅ **Preserved in:**

- Historical trip data
- Assignment records
- Generated reports
- Trip completion data

✅ **Visible in:**

- Inactive Drivers page
- Historical trip records
- Past reports

<Info>
  Disabled drivers are like "archived" - they exist in the system but marked as
  inactive.
</Info>

## Re-enabling Drivers

If you need to bring a driver back:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Inactive Drivers">
    Navigate to **Drivers** → **Inactive Drivers**
  </Step>

<Step title="Find the Driver">Locate the disabled driver in the list</Step>

<Step title="Click Enable">
  Click the **Enable** button on the driver card
  <img src="/images/enable-driver-button.png" alt="Enable Driver Button" />
  Or click **⋮** → **Enable** from menu
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm">Confirm you want to re-enable</Step>

  <Step title="Verify">
    - Driver moves back to Add/Manage Drivers list - Disappears from Inactive
    Drivers - Available in trip dropdowns again - All historical data intact
  </Step>
</Steps>

### When to Re-enable

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Return from Leave">
    **Scenario:** Driver back from medical leave or suspension **Action:**
    Re-enable to resume operations **Verify:** Driver cleared to work, documents
    current
  </Tab>

<Tab title="Season Starts">
  **Scenario:** School year begins, peak season starts **Action:** Re-enable
  seasonal drivers **Verify:** Licenses renewed, ready for work
</Tab>

<Tab title="Rehire">
  **Scenario:** Previously employed driver returns **Action:** Re-enable old
  profile OR create new one **Consider:** If long gap (1+ year), create new
  profile
</Tab>

  <Tab title="Error Correction">
    **Scenario:** Disabled wrong driver by mistake **Action:** Immediately
    re-enable **Note:** Check no trips were incorrectly assigned meanwhile
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Deleting Disabled Drivers

<Warning>**Danger Zone:** Deleting is permanent and destroys all data!</Warning>

You _can_ delete drivers from the Inactive Drivers page, but **only do this if:**

✅ Test driver with zero trips  
✅ Duplicate entry never used  
✅ Created by mistake with no data

❌ **Never delete if:**

- Has any trip assignments
- Has historical data
- Appeared in any reports
- Unsure if they have data

### How to Delete (Use with Extreme Caution)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Inactive Drivers">Ensure driver is already disabled</Step>

<Step title="Verify No Trip Data">
  Check that trip count is zero Confirm no historical assignments exist
</Step>

<Step title="Click Delete">Click **⋮** → **Delete** on the driver</Step>

<Step title="Confirm Multiple Times">
  Read warnings carefully Confirm you understand data will be lost
</Step>

  <Step title="Final Confirmation">
    Type driver name if prompted Click final delete button
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Impact on Reports

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Historical Reports">
    **Existing reports:** - Remain unchanged - Still show disabled driver data -
    Static snapshots preserved **Why?** Reports are historical records
  </Tab>

<Tab title="New Reports">
  **Future reports:** - Can still include disabled driver's trips - If filtering
  by date, disabled driver assignments appear - Historical accuracy maintained
  **Why?** Trip assignment data is preserved
</Tab>

  <Tab title="Trip Lists">
    **Expenditure page:** - Historical trips still show driver name - No
    indication driver is now disabled - This is intentional for accuracy
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I disable multiple drivers at once?" icon="layer-group">
    **Answer:** Yes! In the Disable Driver dialog, check multiple boxes and
    confirm once.
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens to trips already assigned?" icon="route">
  **Answer:** Nothing - they remain intact Historical trip assignments are
  preserved exactly as recorded
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can Staff disable drivers?" icon="user">
  **Answer:** No, Admin only Staff can request Admin to disable drivers as
  needed
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long should I keep disabled drivers?" icon="clock">
  **Recommendation:** - Minimum: 1 full year - Better: 3-5 years - Best:
  Indefinitely (minimal storage impact) **Why?** Disputes, audits, legal
  requirements, historical analysis
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I see which trips a disabled driver did?" icon="list">
    **Answer:** Yes - Go to Expenditure page - Filter by date range when driver
    was active - Driver name still appears in trip list - All data accessible
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't find disabled driver" icon="magnifying-glass">
    **Check:** 1. Are you in Inactive Drivers page (not Add/Manage)? 2. Use
    search function 3. Clear any filters **If not found:** May not be disabled -
    check Add/Manage Drivers
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="Disable button not appearing" icon="ban">
  **Cause:** Logged in as Staff, not Admin **Solution:** Request Admin to
  disable the driver
</Accordion>

  <Accordion
    title="Accidentally disabled wrong driver"
    icon="triangle-exclamation"
  >
    **Solution:** Immediately re-enable them 1. Click **Enable** on the driver
    2. Confirm 3. Driver returns to active status **Impact:** Minimal if caught
    quickly
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
