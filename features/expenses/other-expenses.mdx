Not every cost is fuel. The Others tab in the Expenses section captures all the non-fuel costs that quietly eat into your margins — clerk fees, Milage fees, parking, repairs, and extra costs. Tracking these properly is how you find the hidden losses in your operation.

## What You See on the Others Tab

Open **Expenses**, then click the **Others** tab. You will find:

- All non-fuel expense entries pulled from recorded trips
- **Total Others Cost** displayed at the top right
- A breakdown of each expense category
- A date filter to analyze any specific period

## The Five Non-Fuel Cost Categories

### Clerk Fee

Payments to depot clerks who assist with container clearance at ports and depots. This cost is most relevant for Trailer Trucks handling containerized cargo.

Typical range: KES 1,000 - 5,000 per clearance.

### Milage Fee

Allowances paid to long-distance drivers as compensation for extended trips away from home. This is a driver welfare cost, not a vehicle running cost.

Typical range: KES 500 - 3,000 per trip depending on distance.

### Parking Cost

Any parking fees incurred during a trip — depot parking, overnight parking for long-haul routes, or waiting charges at delivery points.

### Repair Cost

Garage bills, roadside repairs, tyre replacements, and other maintenance expenses that occur during or because of a trip. This is one of the most important categories to monitor over time.

High repair costs on a specific vehicle are a strong signal that it may need major servicing — or replacement.

### Extra Cost

A catch-all for legitimate costs that do not fit other categories: car wash fees, traffic fines, weighbridge fees, loading/offloading charges, and similar expenses.

<Tip>
Use Extra Cost consistently for the same type of expense. If you record weighbridge fees there for some trips but not others, your totals will be unreliable. Train staff to categorize consistently.
</Tip>

## How to Filter and Analyze

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Others tab">
    Go to **Expenses**, then click the **Others** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your date range">
    Enter a start and end date to focus on a specific period.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the totals">
    The combined total at the top right updates automatically. Scroll through entries to spot anything unusual.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Use Cases

**Monitoring repair cost trends:**
Filter the Others tab to the last three months and note the repair cost entries. If one vehicle keeps appearing with high repair costs, it is likely costing more than it earns. Cross-reference with the Dashboard's Vehicle Performance section.

**Reviewing driver allowances:**
At month end, filter to the full month and review Milage fee totals. Make sure allowances are being recorded accurately and match what drivers were actually paid.

**Port and depot cost analysis:**
For businesses running Trailer Trucks, filter by a date range and review clerk fees. These costs are easy to overlook but add up quickly on high-volume months.

**Investigating a costly trip:**
If a vehicle's profitability looks low on the Dashboard, open this tab, filter to the relevant period, and look for that vehicle's entries. A single large repair cost can swing a month from profit to loss.

## Tips for Managing These Costs

<Tip>
Repair costs should always be recorded against the trip they occurred on, even if the garage bill arrives days later. Edit the trip in Expenditure once you have the final amount.
</Tip>

<Warning>
If repair costs are being missed or recorded inconsistently, your per-vehicle profit figures on the Dashboard will be overstated. A vehicle that looks profitable may actually be losing money once true repair costs are factored in.
</Warning>
