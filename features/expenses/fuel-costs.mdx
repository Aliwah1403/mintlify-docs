Fuel is almost always the single largest expense in a logistics operation. The Fuel tab in Expenses gives you a dedicated view of every fuel cost recorded, plus tools to track discounts from partner stations — so you always know your true fuel spend.

## What You See on the Fuel Tab

Open **Expenses**, then click the **Fuel** tab. You will find:

- Every fuel expense entry from recorded trips
- **Total Fuel Cost** — the gross amount spent on fuel
- **Total Fuel Discount** — savings received from discounted fuel purchases
- **Net fuel spend** — what you actually paid after discounts
- A date filter to focus on any specific period

## How Fuel Costs Get Here

Fuel costs are entered as part of trip recording in the Expenditure section. Every time a trip is saved with a fuel amount, it automatically appears in this tab.

There is no separate fuel entry step — it all flows from the trip record.

<Tip>
Make sure drivers or staff report the exact fuel amount spent per trip, not a rounded estimate. Even a KES 500 difference per trip adds up significantly across a fleet over a month.
</Tip>

## Fuel Discounts

If your fleet fuels at partner stations that offer discounted rates, Travada lets you record those discounts in bulk and automatically calculates your savings.

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Adding fuel discounts requires Admin privileges. Staff users can view discount totals but cannot add or edit them.
</Note>

### How Bulk Fuel Discounts Work

Instead of entering discounts trip by trip, you add them in a single bulk entry. You will need:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| Standard fuel price (KES) | The normal pump price per litre |
| Discounted fuel price (KES) | The rate your station charges you |
| Liters | Auto-calculated based on total cost |
| Total cost | Auto-filled |
| Calculated discount | Auto-generated by the system |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Fuel tab">
    Go to **Expenses**, then click the **Fuel** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add Fuel Discount">
    Look for the fuel discount entry option on the page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the standard and discounted prices">
    Input the normal pump price and the discounted rate you received. Travada calculates litres and the total discount automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the entry">
    The discount is added to your totals. Your Total Fuel Discount figure updates immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reading the Discount Summary

Once discounts are recorded, the Fuel tab shows you:

- **Total Fuel Cost** — what you would have paid at standard prices
- **Total Fuel Discount** — the total amount saved
- **Net fuel spend** — your actual cost

For example: if your fleet spent KES 180,000 on fuel in January and received KES 22,000 in discounts, your net fuel spend was KES 158,000. That KES 22,000 goes straight to your bottom line.

## Common Fuel Tracking Scenarios

**Weekly fuel review:**
Filter the Fuel tab to the current week. Compare this week's total against last week to spot unusual consumption — a sharp rise often signals a vehicle issue or route change.

**Measuring discount value:**
At month end, check your Total Fuel Discount figure. This is the direct saving your partnership with a fuel station is delivering. Use it to justify or renegotiate the relationship.

**High fuel cost investigation:**
If total fuel cost is higher than expected, cross-reference with the Expenditure section. Look for trips where the fuel amount seems disproportionate to the destination distance.

## Tips for Fuel Cost Accuracy

<Tip>
Record fuel costs at the time of fueling, not at the end of the week. Drivers often forget exact amounts if left to report later.
</Tip>

<Warning>
Do not combine fuel costs from multiple trips into one entry. Each trip should have its own fuel figure so that per-trip profitability calculations stay accurate.
</Warning>
