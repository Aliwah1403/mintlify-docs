The Fuel tab is dedicated to tracking your single largest expense category - fuel. For most logistics companies, fuel accounts for 40-60% of total operating costs, making it critical to monitor closely.

This tab not only shows fuel expenses but also tracks fuel discounts from partner stations, helping you see actual savings.

## What You See on the Fuel Tab

**At the top right, two key totals:**

1. **Total Fuel Cost** - All fuel expenses across all trips
2. **Total Fuel Discount** - All savings from discounted fuel purchases

**In the main table:**
- Vehicle registration
- Driver name
- Destination
- Trip date
- Standard fuel cost (what you entered when recording the trip)
- Fuel discount (if applied)
- Net fuel cost (standard cost minus discount)

Each row represents one trip's fuel expenses.

## Understanding Fuel Costs

### Standard Fuel Cost

This is the amount you paid at the pump, recorded when you enter a trip in Expenditure.

**Example:**
- Driver fills up KCA 123A with 60 liters at KES 200/liter
- Total paid: KES 12,000
- You record KES 12,000 as fuel cost
- This amount appears in the Fuel tab

### Fuel Discounts

If you have agreements with fuel stations for discounted rates, you can track the savings separately.

**Example scenario:**
- Standard price: KES 200/liter
- Your discounted price: KES 190/liter
- Filled 60 liters
- You paid: KES 11,400 (60 × 190)
- Standard would be: KES 12,000 (60 × 200)
- Your discount: KES 600

**How to record this:**
1. Enter the actual amount paid (KES 11,400) when recording the trip
2. Later, apply the discount (KES 600) in the Fuel tab
3. System shows: Fuel cost KES 11,400, Discount KES 600

This lets you track both what you actually spent AND how much you saved.

## Why Track Fuel Separately?

Fuel deserves special attention because:

### 1. It's Your Biggest Expense

For a typical fleet:
- Fuel: 45% of operating costs
- Repairs: 20%
- Driver allowances: 15%
- Other: 20%

Reducing fuel costs by even 5% has massive impact on profitability.

### 2. It's Variable and Controllable

Unlike fixed costs, fuel expenses vary based on:
- Route efficiency
- Driver behavior
- Vehicle maintenance
- Fuel station choice

You can actually influence fuel costs through better management.

### 3. Discount Opportunities

Many fuel stations offer:
- Corporate discount programs
- Volume-based pricing
- Loyalty card savings
- Partner station networks

Tracking discounts proves the value of these programs.

## Adding Fuel Discounts

There are two ways to record fuel discounts in Travada:

### Method 1: Individual Trip Discounts

Apply a discount to a specific trip:

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Only Admin users can add fuel discounts. Staff users can view discount data but cannot apply discounts.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the trip">
    Locate the trip in the Fuel tab that received a discount
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Add Discount">
    Look for the discount icon or button next to that trip
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter discount amount">
    Input the discount in KES (e.g., 600 for KES 600 saved)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click Save - the discount appears immediately
  </Step>
</Steps>

The total fuel discount updates automatically.

### Method 2: Bulk Fuel Discounts

For multiple trips from the same station with the same discount rate, use bulk entry.

This feature is covered in detail in [Fuel Discounts](/features/expenses/fuel-discounts), but here's the quick overview:

**You enter:**
- Standard fuel price per liter (KES)
- Total liters purchased
- Discounted price per liter (KES)

**System calculates:**
- Standard total cost
- Discounted total cost
- Total savings

**Then you can:**
- Apply this discount to multiple trips at once
- See cumulative savings
- Generate discount reports (coming in future versions)

## Analyzing Fuel Efficiency

Use the Fuel tab to spot efficiency issues:

### Rising Fuel Costs Per Trip

**What to look for:**
Compare fuel costs for similar routes over time.

**Example:**
- Nairobi-Mombasa in January: Average KES 14,000
- Nairobi-Mombasa in March: Average KES 16,500
- Increase: 18% for the same route

**Possible causes:**
- Fuel price increases (check national rates)
- Vehicle maintenance issues (engine efficiency declining)
- Driver behavior changes (aggressive driving, idling)
- Route changes (detours, traffic)

### Vehicle Fuel Consumption Comparison

**What to do:**

1. Filter to one month
2. Calculate fuel cost per trip for each vehicle
3. Compare across similar vehicle types

**Example findings:**

| Vehicle | Trips | Total Fuel | Cost/Trip |
|---------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| KCA 123A (Truck) | 15 | KES 75,000 | KES 5,000 |
| KBZ 456C (Truck) | 14 | KES 84,000 | KES 6,000 |
| KDA 789D (Truck) | 16 | KES 80,000 | KES 5,000 |

**Conclusion:** KBZ 456C consumes 20% more fuel than similar vehicles. Investigate mechanical issues or driver behavior.

### Seasonal Patterns

**What to track:**

Compare fuel costs across different months:

- Are costs higher in rainy season? (slower traffic, detours)
- Do certain months have more fuel-intensive routes?
- How do holiday periods affect consumption?

This helps with:
- Budget forecasting
- Route planning
- Seasonal staffing

## Fuel Discount Tracking Benefits

Why bother tracking discounts separately?

### 1. Prove Program Value

"We saved KES 45,000 in fuel discounts last quarter by using Partner Station X exclusively"

This justifies:
- Continuing discount agreements
- Negotiating better rates
- Directing drivers to specific stations

### 2. Driver Compliance Monitoring

If you have agreements with specific stations, tracking discounts shows whether drivers are actually using those stations.

**Red flag:** Fuel costs without discounts when they should have discounts
**Likely cause:** Driver filled up at non-partner station
**Action:** Remind drivers of fuel station policy

### 3. Financial Reporting Accuracy

For accounting and tax purposes:
- Gross fuel expense: What you would have paid
- Net fuel expense: What you actually paid
- Discount: The difference (may be tax-deductible)

Proper tracking ensures accurate financial statements.

## Common Fuel Tracking Workflows

### Weekly Fuel Review

**Who:** Operations manager  
**When:** Monday mornings  
**Process:**

1. Filter Fuel tab to last 7 days
2. Check total fuel cost vs. budget
3. Review any unusually high trips
4. Verify discounts were applied where expected
5. Flag issues for follow-up with drivers

### Monthly Fuel Analysis

**Who:** Owner or fleet manager  
**When:** Month-end  
**Process:**

1. Filter to previous month
2. Calculate average fuel cost per trip
3. Compare to previous month
4. Identify vehicles with rising fuel costs
5. Check total discounts received
6. Generate fuel cost report
7. Adjust next month's budget if needed

### Quarterly Efficiency Assessment

**Who:** CFO or senior management  
**When:** End of each quarter  
**Process:**

1. Filter to 3-month period
2. Calculate fuel cost as % of revenue
3. Compare to industry benchmarks (typically 40-50%)
4. Review discount program effectiveness
5. Identify underperforming vehicles
6. Make strategic decisions (vehicle replacement, route changes, driver training)

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Total fuel cost seems too high">
    Verify you're looking at the right date range. If the total still seems wrong, check individual trip entries in Expenditure - someone may have entered fuel costs incorrectly (extra zeros, wrong amounts).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Discount isn't showing after I added it">
    Refresh the page. If it still doesn't appear, check that you saved the discount. Also verify you have Admin permissions - Staff users cannot add discounts.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I see fuel costs per kilometer?">
    Not directly in this tab, but you can calculate it: Total fuel cost ÷ total kilometers. Future versions may add this metric automatically.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How do I track fuel purchased vs fuel used?">
    Travada currently tracks fuel purchased (costs). If you want to track consumption vs purchases for theft prevention, you'll need external tracking (fuel cards or manual logs).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<Tip>
**Fuel tracking tips:**
- Enter fuel costs immediately after trips (don't batch-enter days later)
- Always include fuel receipts with trip documentation
- Apply discounts within 24 hours of trip entry
- Review fuel anomalies weekly, not monthly
- Compare similar routes across drivers to spot efficiency issues
- Track fuel costs per vehicle per month to identify maintenance needs
</Tip>
