The "All" tab in the Expenses section gives you a complete view of every cost your fleet has incurred. This is your go-to place for understanding total spending and generating expense reports.

## What You See on the All Expenses Tab

When you open the All tab, you'll see:

**At the top right:**
- **Total Expense** - Sum of all costs in the current view

**In the main table:**
- Vehicle registration
- Driver name
- Destination
- Trip date
- All expense categories:
  - Fuel cost
  - Clerk fee
  - Milage fee
  - Parking cost
  - Repair cost
  - Extra cost
- Total cost per trip (sum of all categories)

Each row represents one trip with all its associated costs.

## Understanding the Total Expense

The total expense amount at the top right updates dynamically based on your filters.

**Example:**
- **Without filters:** Shows total of ALL expenses in the system
- **Filtered to January 2026:** Shows only January expenses
- **Filtered to KCA 123A:** Shows only that vehicle's expenses
- **Filtered to fuel only:** Shows total fuel across all trips

This makes it easy to answer questions like:
- "How much did we spend in total last month?" → KES 1,250,000
- "What were KBZ 456C's total costs?" → KES 340,000
- "How much have we spent on parking this year?" → KES 45,000

## Filtering Expense Data

Use filters to narrow down exactly what you want to see.

### By Date Range

The most common filter - see expenses for a specific period:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the date filter">
    Look for the date range selector at the top of the page
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select start date">
    Choose when you want the period to begin
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select end date">
    Choose when you want the period to end
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Apply filter">
    Click Apply or the filter icon - the table and total update automatically
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Common date filters:**
- Last 7 days - Quick weekly review
- Last 30 days - Monthly analysis
- Custom range - Specific billing periods or project timelines

### By Vehicle

See expenses for a specific vehicle:

1. Click the vehicle filter dropdown
2. Select the registration number
3. All expenses for that vehicle appear
4. Total shows that vehicle's total spend

**Use case:** "I want to see all costs for KCA 123A to calculate its profitability"

### By Expense Category

Filter to see only specific types of costs:

- Show only fuel expenses
- Show only repair costs
- Show only parking fees
- Combine multiple categories

**Use case:** "Show me all fuel and repair costs for January to compare maintenance spending"

### Combining Filters

You can apply multiple filters simultaneously:

**Example 1: Vehicle + Date**
- Vehicle: KBZ 789C
- Date: January 1-31, 2026
- Result: All expenses for KBZ 789C in January

**Example 2: Category + Date**
- Category: Fuel
- Date: Last 7 days
- Result: All fuel expenses in the past week

**Example 3: Vehicle + Category + Date**
- Vehicle: KCA 123A
- Category: Repair cost
- Date: Last 6 months
- Result: All repair expenses for KCA 123A in the past 6 months

## Sorting Expense Data

Click column headers to sort:

- **By date** - See oldest or newest expenses first
- **By amount** - Identify highest-cost trips
- **By vehicle** - Group expenses by vehicle
- **By destination** - See costs by route

Sorting helps you quickly spot:
- Which trips were most expensive
- Unusual cost patterns
- Trends over time

## Generating Expense Reports

The All tab lets you create simplified expense reports for management or accounting.

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Only Admin users can generate expense reports. Staff can view data but cannot create reports.
</Note>

### How to Generate a Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Apply your filters">
    First, filter to show exactly what you want in the report (specific date range, vehicle, etc.)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Generate Report">
    Look for the "Generate Report" or "Create Report" button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter report name">
    Give your report a descriptive name like "January 2026 Fleet Expenses" or "KCA 123A Q1 Costs"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create">
    The system generates the report and saves it to the Reports section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access the report">
    Find your report in the Reports section to view, download, or share
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What's included in the report:**
- Report name and creation date
- Date range covered
- All expense data matching your filters
- Total expenses
- Breakdown by category
- Professional formatting for sharing

### Report Naming Best Practices

Good report names are:

<Tip>
**Clear and specific:**
- ✅ "January 2026 All Fleet Expenses"
- ✅ "KBZ 789C Repair Costs Q1 2026"
- ✅ "Weekly Fuel Report Week of Jan 20"
- ❌ "Report 1"
- ❌ "Expenses"
- ❌ "Test"
</Tip>

Include:
- Time period (January, Q1, Week of Jan 20)
- What it covers (All Fleet, KBZ 789C, Fuel Only)
- Year (2026)

This makes reports easy to find later when you have dozens of them.

## Common Workflows

### Daily Review

**Who:** Operations manager  
**When:** End of each day  
**How:**
1. Filter to today's date
2. Review all expenses entered
3. Verify amounts look reasonable
4. Flag any unusual costs for follow-up

### Weekly Reconciliation

**Who:** Finance team  
**When:** Every Monday morning  
**How:**
1. Filter to previous week (Mon-Sun)
2. Compare total to bank statements
3. Identify any missing entries
4. Generate weekly expense report
5. Share with management

### Monthly Analysis

**Who:** Owner or CFO  
**When:** First week of each month  
**How:**
1. Filter to previous month
2. Review total spend vs budget
3. Identify highest-cost categories
4. Compare to same month last year
5. Generate monthly expense report
6. Use insights for budget planning

### Vehicle Performance Review

**Who:** Fleet manager  
**When:** Quarterly  
**How:**
1. Filter to one vehicle at a time
2. Review 3-month expense total
3. Compare across all vehicles
4. Identify vehicles with rising costs
5. Make decisions about repairs vs replacement

2. Look for "Export" or "Download" button
3. Choose format (CSV, Excel, PDF)
4. Open the downloaded file
5. Import into your accounting system

**Common export uses:**
- Import into QuickBooks or other accounting software

- Archive for record-keeping

## Understanding Expense Timing

When do expenses appear in this section?

**Immediately after trip entry:**
As soon as someone records a trip in Expenditure with costs, those costs appear here instantly. There's no delay or approval process.

**Example timeline:**
- 9:00 AM: Driver completes trip
- 10:30 AM: Staff records trip with KES 8,000 fuel cost
- 10:30 AM: KES 8,000 appears in All Expenses tab
- 10:31 AM: Total Expense increases by KES 8,000

This real-time visibility helps you stay on top of spending as it happens.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Total doesn't match what I expected">
    Check your filters - you might be viewing a subset of data. Clear all filters to see the true total, then reapply filters one by one to isolate the issue.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="I can't find a specific expense">
    The trip might be recorded with a different date or vehicle than you remember. Try clearing filters and sorting by amount to find high-value expenses, or search by destination.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I edit expenses here?">
    No. Expenses are read-only views of trip data. To change an expense amount, you must edit (or delete and re-record) the original trip in the Expenditure section.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Why can't I generate a report?">
    Report generation is Admin-only. If you're a Staff user, you'll need to ask an Admin to generate the report for you, or request an upgrade to Admin access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips for Effective Expense Tracking

<Tip>
**Monthly routine:**
1. Week 1: Review last month's total expenses
2. Week 2: Compare to budget and identify variances
3. Week 3: Analyze category breakdowns for trends
4. Week 4: Generate monthly report for stakeholders
</Tip>

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Sudden spikes in repair costs (vehicle deteriorating?)
- Fuel costs increasing faster than trip volume (efficiency declining?)
- Extra costs growing (lack of cost control?)
- One vehicle's expenses much higher than others (problem vehicle?)
