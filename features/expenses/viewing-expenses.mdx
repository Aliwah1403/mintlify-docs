The All Expenses tab gives you a single, unified view of every cost recorded across your fleet — fuel, repairs, parking, clerk fees, and everything in between. It is your go-to view for totals, reconciliation, and full-picture cost analysis.

## What You See on This Page

When you open the All Expenses tab, you will see:

- A list of every expense entry, pulled from trips recorded in Expenditure
- The **total expense figure** displayed at the top right, updating automatically as you filter
- Each entry showing the vehicle, trip date, destination, and cost breakdown
- A date range filter to narrow results to any period

## How to Filter Expenses

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Expenses section">
    From the main menu, click **Expenses**, then select the **All** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your date range">
    Enter a start date and end date in the date filter fields (e.g., 01 Jan 2026 - 31 Jan 2026).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the results">
    The table updates to show only expenses within that period. The total at the top right recalculates automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
For month-end reporting, set the filter to the first and last day of the month. The total shown is the exact figure you need for your accounts.
</Tip>

## Understanding the Expense Total

The total displayed at the top right represents the sum of **all expense categories** for your selected period:

- Fuel Cost
- Clerk Fee
- Milage Fee
- Parking Cost
- Repair Cost
- Extra Cost

This is your true total cost of operations for that period, before accounting for income.

## Generating an Expense Report

Once you have filtered to the period you need, you can generate a formal expense report.

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Only Admin users can generate expense reports. Staff users can view and filter the data but cannot create reports.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filter to the period you need">
    Set your date range so the table shows the correct expenses.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Generate Report">
    Look for the report generation option above the expense table.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter a report name">
    Give it a descriptive name, for example: "Fleet Expenses - January 2026".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save and access the report">
    The report is saved to your Reports section where it can be opened, shared, or deleted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Generated reports have a default expiry of 30 days. You can find and manage all reports in the [Reports section](/features/reports/overview).

## Common Use Cases

**Reconciling with bank statements:**
Set the date range to match your bank statement period. Compare the total in Travada against your actual bank outflows to verify accuracy.

**Preparing for an audit:**
Filter to the full financial year and generate a report. The structured format makes it easy to hand over to an accountant.

**Spotting unusual costs:**
Scroll through entries for the month and look for trips with unusually high totals. These often signal a repair or incident worth investigating.

**Comparing months:**
Run the filter for this month, note the total, then run it for last month. A quick way to see whether your cost base is growing.

## Tips for Accurate Data

<Tip>
Expenses are only as accurate as the trips recorded in Expenditure. If staff are recording trips late or missing costs, your totals here will be incomplete. Encourage same-day trip entry for the most reliable data.
</Tip>

<Warning>
If you notice a cost missing from this view, it means the trip was either not recorded yet or the cost field was left blank during entry. Go to Expenditure to find and edit the trip.
</Warning>
