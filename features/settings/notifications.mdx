Notification settings let you control which alerts Travada sends to your email. Whether you want every update or just critical alerts, you can customize your notification preferences here.

Unlike appearance settings, notification preferences are **personal** - each user controls their own alerts independently.

## Types of Notifications

Travada offers four categories of notifications:

### 1. Email Notifications (Master Toggle)

This is the master switch for all email alerts. When enabled, you'll receive emails based on your other preferences below. When disabled, Travada sends no emails at all.

**Use cases:**
- **Enable:** You want to stay updated via email
- **Disable:** You prefer checking Travada directly and don't want emails

### 2. General System Updates

Notifications about platform changes and important announcements:

- New feature releases
- System maintenance schedules
- Important platform updates
- Security announcements
- Terms of service changes

**Frequency:** Occasional (typically 1-2 per month)

**Example email:**
> "New Feature: Bulk Fuel Discount Entry Now Available"
> "Scheduled Maintenance: Sunday 2AM-4AM EAT"

<Tip>
Keep this enabled unless you have a dedicated admin who monitors updates. Missing important system announcements can affect your workflow.
</Tip>

### 3. Report Generation Alerts

Get notified when reports are created, with direct links to view them:

- Rate report completion
- Expense report completion
- Report name and creator
- Direct link to open the report
- Expiry date reminder

**Frequency:** Based on your report generation activity

**Example email:**
> "Your report 'Artcaffe February Invoice' is ready"
> "Created by: admin@yourcompany.com"
> "View Report: [Direct Link]"
> "Expires: March 15, 2026"

**Who should enable this:**
- Finance teams generating client invoices
- Managers who create monthly expense reports
- Anyone who generates reports for others to review

**Who can disable this:**
- Users who generate reports and immediately download them
- Teams that prefer checking the Reports section directly

### 4. Weekly Analytics and Usage Summaries

Every Monday morning, receive a summary of the previous week:

- Total trips completed
- Total income and expenses
- Top performing vehicles
- Most frequent destinations
- Cost trends and highlights
- Notable changes vs previous week

**Frequency:** Once per week (Monday mornings)

**Example email:**
> "Week of Jan 20-26, 2026 Summary"
> "📊 15 trips completed (+3 from previous week)"
> "💰 Income: KES 450,000 | Expenses: KES 280,000"
> "🚛 Top vehicle: KCA 123A (KES 85,000 profit)"
> "⛽ Fuel costs up 12% - investigate route efficiency"

<Tip>
Weekly summaries are great for owners and managers who don't log in daily but want to stay informed about business performance.
</Tip>

## How to Configure Notifications

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click the gear icon and select **Account → Notifications**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review current settings">
    You'll see toggles for each notification type with their current status
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable or disable">
    Click each toggle to turn notifications on or off
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Changes save automatically - no submit button needed
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recommended Settings by Role

Different team members benefit from different notification setups:

### For Company Owners

```
✅ Email Notifications: ON
✅ General System Updates: ON
❌ Report Generation Alerts: OFF (unless you generate reports yourself)
✅ Weekly Analytics Summaries: ON
```

**Why:** Stay informed about business performance without daily involvement. Weekly summaries give you the big picture.

### For Finance/Admin Staff

```
✅ Email Notifications: ON
✅ General System Updates: ON
✅ Report Generation Alerts: ON
❌ Weekly Analytics Summaries: OFF (you see this data daily anyway)
```

**Why:** You need immediate notification when reports are ready for clients. System updates help you plan around maintenance.

### For Operations Managers

```
✅ Email Notifications: ON
✅ General System Updates: ON
❌ Report Generation Alerts: OFF
✅ Weekly Analytics Summaries: ON
```

**Why:** Weekly summaries help you spot operational trends. You're in the system daily so don't need report alerts.

### For Data Entry Staff

```
❌ Email Notifications: OFF
or
✅ Email Notifications: ON
✅ General System Updates: ON
❌ Report Generation Alerts: OFF
❌ Weekly Analytics Summaries: OFF
```

**Why:** Staff who only record trips don't need most notifications. If enabled, just system updates for platform changes.

## Understanding Email Delivery

### When Emails Are Sent

- **System updates:** As needed, typically during business hours
- **Report alerts:** Immediately when report is generated
- **Weekly summaries:** Every Monday at 6:00 AM EAT

### Email Address Used

Travada sends notifications to the email address associated with your account. To change your email:

1. Go to **Account Settings** (covered in [Account Settings](/features/settings/account))
2. Update your email address
3. Verify the new email
4. Notifications will now go to the new address

### If You're Not Receiving Emails

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Check your spam folder">
    Travada emails might be filtered as spam. Look for emails from notifications@travadasys.com and mark them as "Not Spam" or add to your contacts.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Verify email notifications are enabled">
    Go to Settings → Account → Notifications and ensure the master "Email Notifications" toggle is ON.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Confirm your email address is correct">
    Check your account profile to ensure the email on file is current and spelled correctly.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Check with your IT team">
    Some corporate email systems block external notifications. Ask your IT team to whitelist notifications@travadasys.com.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Notification Best Practices

### Start Conservative

When first setting up:

1. Enable all notifications initially
2. Use the system for 2-3 weeks
3. Disable notifications you find unnecessary
4. Adjust based on your actual usage patterns

### Weekly Summary Tips

The weekly summary is most useful when:

- You review it the same time each week (Monday morning routine)
- You compare trends month-over-month
- You use it to identify issues before they become problems
- You share insights with your team in Monday meetings

### Report Alert Strategy

Enable report alerts if:

- You generate reports for clients and need confirmation they're ready
- Multiple people generate reports and you need to track what's created
- Reports have tight deadlines and you can't afford to miss completion

Disable report alerts if:

- You generate a report and immediately download it
- You prefer checking the Reports section manually
- You're generating many test reports and don't want email clutter

## Privacy and Data

Important to know:

- Notification preferences are stored securely
- Email content contains summary data only, not sensitive financial details
- Weekly summaries show aggregated totals, not individual trip details
- Report alert emails link to Travada, they don't include the full report

All emails require you to log in to Travada to see detailed information.

## Managing Email Overload

If you're getting too many emails:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the source">
    Check which notification type is sending the most emails
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Disable non-critical alerts">
    Turn off notifications you don't actively use
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Keep system updates enabled">
    Even if you disable everything else, keep system updates on for critical announcements
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Consider weekly summaries only">
    Instead of daily engagement, rely on Monday morning summaries
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Changes Take Effect Immediately

When you toggle a notification setting:

- The change saves automatically
- Future emails follow the new preference
- No confirmation email is sent
- You can change settings as often as needed

## Team Coordination

If you have multiple team members:

<Tip>
**Suggested approach:** Have at least one person with all notifications enabled as a "backup" to ensure critical updates aren't missed by the entire team.
</Tip>

For example:
- Owner: Weekly summaries only
- Admin: All notifications enabled (backup person)
- Manager: System updates + weekly summaries
- Staff: System updates only

This ensures coverage without everyone getting overwhelmed.
