This guide walks you through the complete lifecycle of a trip in Travada - from the moment a vehicle leaves on a delivery until the financial data appears in your reports. Follow this workflow to ensure accurate, complete trip records.

## Overview: The Trip Lifecycle

A complete trip workflow involves five stages:

1. **Pre-trip setup** - Ensure vehicle and driver exist in the system
2. **Trip execution** - Driver completes the delivery
3. **Trip recording** - Enter trip details and costs into Travada
4. **Verification** - Confirm data appears correctly across all sections
5. **Analysis** - Review trip profitability and performance

**Time required:** 5-10 minutes per trip (after initial setup)

## Stage 1: Pre-Trip Setup

Before you can record any trip, verify the vehicle and driver are in Travada.

### Check if Vehicle Exists

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Vehicles page">
    Click **Vehicles** in the main navigation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search for the vehicle">
    Look for the registration number (e.g., KCA 123A)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="If not found, add it">
    Click **+ Add Vehicle**, enter registration, select vehicle type and ownership status
    
    See [Adding a Vehicle](/getting-started/adding-vehicles) for details
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Check if Driver Exists

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Drivers page">
    Click **Drivers** in the main navigation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search for the driver">
    Look for the driver's name
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="If not found, add them">
    Click **+ Add Driver**, enter name and phone number
    
    See [Adding a Driver](/getting-started/adding-drivers) for details
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Only Admin users can add vehicles and drivers. If you're Staff, ask an Admin to add them before you can record the trip.
</Note>

**Do this once:** After initial setup, you won't need to add the same vehicle/driver again unless your fleet changes.

## Stage 2: Trip Execution

The driver completes the actual delivery. During or after the trip, gather this information:

### Required Information Checklist

**Trip Identification:**
- ✅ Vehicle registration number
- ✅ Driver name
- ✅ Destination (delivery location)
- ✅ Trip date

**Financial Information:**
- ✅ Rate charged to client (KES)
- ✅ Detention fee if applicable (KES)
- ✅ Fuel cost - total paid at pump (KES)
- ✅ Clerk fee if paid (KES)
- ✅ Milage fee if applicable (KES)
- ✅ Parking costs (KES)
- ✅ Any repair costs (KES)
- ✅ Extra costs like car wash, tolls (KES)

**Supporting Documents:**
- Fuel receipt
- Client delivery note (proof of rate)
- Any other receipts for expenses

<Tip>
**Best practice:** Have drivers submit this information immediately after completing the trip, while details are fresh. WhatsApp photos of receipts work well for quick documentation.
</Tip>

## Stage 3: Trip Recording

Now enter the trip into Travada's Expenditure section.

### Recording Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Expenditure">
    Click **Expenditure** in the main menu
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Add Trip">
    Look for the **+ Add Trip** or **Record Trip** button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Page 1: Trip Identification">
    Fill in the first form page:
    - **Vehicle:** Select from dropdown (e.g., KCA 123A)
    - **Driver:** Select from dropdown
    - **Destination:** Type the delivery location (e.g., "Mombasa Port")
    - **Date:** Select the trip date (defaults to today)
    
    Click **Next** to continue
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Page 2: Financial Details">
    Enter all income and expense amounts:
    
    **Income:**
    - **Rate:** KES amount charged to client
    - **Detention Fee:** KES amount for delays (if applicable, otherwise leave at 0)
    
    **Expenses:**
    - **Fuel Cost:** Total fuel expense
    - **Clerk Fee:** Depot/port clerk payment (if applicable)
    - **Milage Fee:** Driver distance allowance (if applicable)
    - **Parking:** Parking fees
    - **Repair Cost:** Any garage expenses
    - **Extra Cost:** Car wash, tolls, etc.
    
    **Important:** Enter actual amounts. Use 0 for categories that don't apply to this trip
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Submit">
    Double-check all amounts before clicking **Submit**
    
    Common mistakes to avoid:
    - Extra zeros (KES 150,000 instead of 15,000)
    - Wrong vehicle selected
    - Incorrect date
    - Missing fuel cost (most common omission)
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Time to complete:** 3-5 minutes per trip with all information ready

### Example: Complete Trip Entry

**Scenario:** KCA 123A delivers goods from Nairobi to Mombasa

**Page 1 - Identification:**
- Vehicle: KCA 123A
- Driver: John Mwangi
- Destination: Mombasa Port
- Date: January 15, 2026

**Page 2 - Financial:**
- Rate: KES 45,000 (what client pays)
- Detention Fee: KES 0 (no delays)
- Fuel Cost: KES 12,000 (60 liters × KES 200)
- Clerk Fee: KES 0 (not a container trip)
- Milage Fee: KES 5,000 (500km × company policy)
- Parking: KES 800 (overnight parking)
- Repair Cost: KES 0 (no repairs needed)
- Extra Cost: KES 500 (car wash)

**Result:**
- Total Income: KES 45,000
- Total Expenses: KES 18,300
- Trip Profit: KES 26,700

## Stage 4: Verification

After submitting, verify the trip appears correctly across Travada.

### Immediate Checks (Within 5 Minutes)

**1. Check Expenditure List**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Stay on Expenditure page">
    After submitting, you should see the trip list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify your trip appears">
    Look for the trip you just entered at the top of the list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm details are correct">
    Check vehicle, driver, destination, date, and amounts match what you entered
  </Step>
</Steps>

**If the trip doesn't appear:** Refresh the page. If still missing, it didn't save - re-enter it.

**2. Check Income Section**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Incomes">
    Click **Incomes** in the main menu
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open the All tab">
    Your trip should appear in the income list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify income amounts">
    Rate and detention fee should match what you entered
    
    Total income = Rate + Detention Fee
  </Step>
</Steps>

**3. Check Expenses Section**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Expenses">
    Click **Expenses** in the main menu
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check All tab">
    Your trip should appear with all expense categories
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify expense amounts">
    All six expense categories should show correct amounts
    
    Total expense = Sum of all six categories
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Dashboard Updates (Within 1 Hour)

**4. Check Dashboard Metrics**

Navigate to the Dashboard and verify:

- **Total Income** increased by your trip's income
- **Total Expenses** increased by your trip's expenses
- **Total Profit** reflects the new trip
- **Trip Completion Trend** shows an additional trip for today
- **Top Destinations** may update if this destination is significant
- **Vehicle Performance** updates for the vehicle you used

<Info>
Dashboard charts may take a few minutes to refresh. If numbers don't update immediately, wait 5 minutes and refresh the page.
</Info>

## Stage 5: Analysis

Once verified, analyze the trip's performance.

### Calculate Trip Profitability

**Formula:**
```
Trip Profit = Total Income - Total Expenses
Profit Margin = (Trip Profit ÷ Total Income) × 100
```

**Example from our Nairobi-Mombasa trip:**
- Total Income: KES 45,000
- Total Expenses: KES 18,300
- Trip Profit: KES 26,700
- Profit Margin: (26,700 ÷ 45,000) × 100 = **59.3%**

**Benchmarks:**
- **Excellent:** > 50% profit margin
- **Good:** 35-50% profit margin
- **Acceptable:** 20-35% profit margin
- **Concerning:** < 20% profit margin

### Identify Issues

**If profit margin is low, check:**

**1. Revenue too low?**
- Rate charged doesn't cover costs
- Missing detention fees
- Client underpaying

**Action:** Renegotiate rates or decline unprofitable routes

**2. Fuel costs too high?**
- Inefficient route
- Vehicle fuel consumption issue
- Fuel prices increased

**Action:** Review route efficiency, check vehicle maintenance

**3. Unexpected expenses?**
- Unplanned repairs
- Higher parking than normal
- Extra costs accumulating

**Action:** Investigate why, plan better for future trips

### Compare to Similar Trips

Look at historical data for the same route:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Expenditure">
    Access the trip list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter by destination">
    Show only trips to the same destination (e.g., "Mombasa Port")
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Compare costs">
    Look at fuel, milage, and total expenses for similar trips
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Identify patterns">
    Is this trip normal, cheaper, or more expensive than usual?
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example findings:**
- Average Nairobi-Mombasa fuel: KES 12,500
- This trip fuel: KES 12,000
- **Conclusion:** Slightly better than average, good fuel efficiency

## Common Trip Types: Specific Workflows

### Container Transport (Trailer Trucks)

**Additional information needed:**
- Container number
- Container size (20ft, 40ft)
- Container weight
- Clerk fee (almost always required)
- Detention fee (common for port delays)

**Recording specifics:**
- Include container details in destination or notes
- Always record clerk fee
- Calculate detention carefully (can be significant revenue)
- May need to generate Rate Report with container details for client invoicing

### Long-Distance Freight

**Additional information needed:**
- Exact distance (for milage fee calculation)
- Overnight parking costs
- Multiple fuel stops

**Recording specifics:**
- Always include milage fee
- Parking fees typically higher
- May have repair costs en route
- Check fuel receipts from multiple stations

### Local/City Deliveries

**Additional information needed:**
- Multiple drops in one trip (how to record)
- Traffic fines if incurred
- Short-distance costs

**Recording specifics:**
- Lower fuel costs
- May have parking fees
- Extra costs for car wash, tolls common
- No milage fees (short distance)
- May need to combine multiple deliveries into one trip record

### Refrigerated Transport (Reefers)

**Additional information needed:**
- Higher fuel consumption (refrigeration)
- Temperature monitoring costs
- Special handling fees

**Recording specifics:**
- Fuel costs typically 30-50% higher than standard trucks
- May have extra costs for refrigeration maintenance
- Premium rates should be charged
- Track fuel per km to monitor refrigeration efficiency

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I made a mistake entering amounts">
    Current limitation: You cannot edit amounts after submission. You must:
    1. Delete the incorrect trip (Admin only)
    2. Re-record with correct information
    
    See [Editing Trip Details](/features/expenditure/editing-trip) for workarounds
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Vehicle or driver doesn't appear in dropdown">
    They haven't been added to the system yet. An Admin must add them first:
    - Go to Vehicles or Drivers page
    - Click Add
    - Enter required information
    - Return to Expenditure to record the trip
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Trip appears in Expenditure but not in Income/Expenses">
    This is unusual. Try:
    1. Refresh the browser page
    2. Wait 5 minutes (rare sync delay)
    3. If still missing, contact support - there may be a data sync issue
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Dashboard didn't update after I added a trip">
    Dashboard charts can take a few minutes to refresh:
    1. Wait 5 minutes
    2. Hard refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac)
    3. Check if your date filter includes today's date
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="I don't have all the expense information yet">
    Options:
    1. **Best:** Wait until you have complete information, then record
    2. **Acceptable:** Enter what you know with zeros for missing amounts, note to update later (but remember: you can't edit amounts after submission)
    3. **Not recommended:** Skip the trip - incomplete data is better than no data
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices for Efficient Trip Recording

### Daily Recording Routine

**Recommended workflow:**

**End of day (5:00 PM - 6:00 PM):**
1. Collect all trip information from drivers (WhatsApp, physical receipts)
2. Record all trips completed that day
3. Verify each trip appears in Expenditure list
4. Note any missing information for follow-up tomorrow

**Benefits:**
- Information is fresh
- Drivers still available for questions
- Receipts don't get lost
- Dashboard stays current

### Batch Entry vs Real-Time

**Real-time entry (as trips complete):**
- ✅ Most accurate
- ✅ Dashboard always current
- ❌ Requires constant attention

**Batch entry (end of day/week):**
- ✅ More efficient (10-20 trips in one session)
- ✅ Better focus
- ❌ Information may be forgotten
- ❌ Receipts may be lost

**Recommendation:** End-of-day batch entry - good balance of accuracy and efficiency

### Quality Control Checklist

Before submitting each trip:

- [ ] Vehicle registration matches delivery documentation
- [ ] Driver name is correct
- [ ] Destination spelling is consistent
- [ ] Date is accurate (not defaulting to today if trip was earlier)
- [ ] Rate matches client invoice or agreement
- [ ] Fuel receipt amount matches entered amount
- [ ] All applicable expense categories have amounts
- [ ] Categories that don't apply show 0 (not blank)
- [ ] Total income and expenses look reasonable
- [ ] Trip should be profitable (unless unusual circumstances)

## Scaling Your Workflow

### Small Fleet (1-5 vehicles)

- One person can handle all trip entry
- Record trips daily
- Monthly review is sufficient
- Focus on accuracy over speed

### Medium Fleet (6-20 vehicles)

- Designate one person for trip entry
- Consider morning and afternoon recording sessions
- Weekly reviews to catch errors
- Implement receipt submission procedures

### Large Fleet (20+ vehicles)

- Multiple people may record trips
- Real-time or twice-daily entry required
- Daily verification essential
- Automated receipt collection systems
- Regular audits to ensure consistency
