Month-end is when you pull together all the trip data from the past 30 days into clear financial reports for stakeholders. This guide walks you through the complete month-end process in Travada, from data verification to report generation and distribution.

## Month-End Overview

**Goal:** Produce accurate financial summaries showing what you earned, spent, and profited for the month.

**Who does this:** Typically Admin or Finance team members

**How long it takes:** 1-2 hours depending on fleet size and complexity

**Frequency:** First 1-3 days of each new month (e.g., run January reports on Feb 1-3)

## The Month-End Process (6 Steps)

1. **Data verification** - Ensure all trips are recorded
2. **Income review** - Confirm all revenue is captured
3. **Expense review** - Verify all costs are entered
4. **Dashboard analysis** - Review performance metrics
5. **Report generation** - Create formal reports
6. **Report distribution** - Share with stakeholders

Let's go through each step in detail.

## Step 1: Data Verification

Before generating reports, confirm all trips for the month are in Travada.

### Check Trip Completeness

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Expenditure">
    Navigate to the Expenditure section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set date filter">
    Filter to the previous month (e.g., January 1-31, 2026)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Count trips">
    Note the total number of trips recorded
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Cross-reference">
    Compare against:
    - Driver trip logs (if kept separately)
    - Client delivery confirmations
    - Fuel receipt count
    - Your expected trip volume
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Identify missing trips">
    If the count is lower than expected, investigate which trips are missing
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Common gaps:**
- Last few days of the month (trips completed but not yet recorded)
- Weekend trips (often forgotten)
- Emergency or unplanned trips
- Trips by part-time drivers

<Tip>
**Best practice:** Maintain a simple trip log (even a notebook or spreadsheet) where drivers note every trip. This gives you a quick reference count to verify against Travada.
</Tip>

### Verify Trip Details

Spot-check 10-20% of trips for accuracy:

1. Pick random trips throughout the month
2. Check each against original documentation (receipts, invoices)
3. Verify:
   - Amounts match receipts
   - Destination is correct
   - Vehicle and driver are accurate
   - Date is right

**Red flags:**
- Round numbers everywhere (KES 10,000, 5,000, 15,000) - might be estimates
- Suspiciously high or low amounts
- Same amount repeated multiple times
- Dates all clustered (suggests batch entry with date errors)

### Add Missing Trips

If you find missing trips:

<Note>
**Important:** You can record trips with past dates. Just ensure you set the correct date when entering the trip so it appears in the right month's data.
</Note>

1. Gather trip information from driver or client
2. Go to Expenditure → Add Trip
3. **Critical:** Set the trip date to when it actually occurred (not today)
4. Enter all details normally
5. Verify it appears in the month you're reporting on

## Step 2: Income Review

Confirm all revenue is captured and accurate.

### Total Income Check

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Incomes">
    Navigate to Incomes section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open All tab">
    This shows complete income picture
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter to the month">
    Set date range to the month you're reporting (e.g., Jan 1-31)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Note the total income">
    Look at the total in the top right corner
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example:**
- Total Income for January 2026: KES 2,450,000

### Break Down by Income Type

Check the two income components:

**1. Rate Income (All tab)**
- Filter to Rate tab
- Note total rate fees
- This should be your primary revenue source

**Example:** KES 2,320,000 in rate fees

**2. Detention Income (Detention tab)**
- Switch to Detention tab
- Note total detention fees
- Should be smaller portion of income

**Example:** KES 130,000 in detention fees

**Verify:** Rate + Detention = Total Income
- KES 2,320,000 + KES 130,000 = KES 2,450,000 ✓

### Client Reconciliation

For each major client, verify income matches what they should owe:

1. Filter income by destination or client name
2. Sum their trips
3. Compare to:
   - Your invoices to them
   - Their payment records
   - Agreed rates × number of trips

**If there's a mismatch:**
- Missing trips for this client?
- Rate entered incorrectly?
- Detention fees not captured?
- They had trips you forgot to record?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Example: Client Reconciliation">
    **Client:** Artcaffe (food deliveries)
    
    **Expected:**
    - 12 trips in January
    - KES 15,000 per trip
    - Total should be: KES 180,000
    
    **Travada shows:**
    - Filter to "Artcaffe" destination
    - 11 trips found
    - Total: KES 165,000
    
    **Issue:** Missing 1 trip
    
    **Action:** Check with driver, find missing trip details, add to Travada before finalizing reports
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step 3: Expense Review

Verify all costs are captured and categorized correctly.

### Total Expenses Check

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Expenses">
    Navigate to Expenses section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open All tab">
    Shows all expense categories combined
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter to the month">
    Set date range to your reporting month
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Note total expenses">
    Check the total in the top right
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example:**
- Total Expenses for January 2026: KES 1,380,000

### Review by Category

Check each expense category for reasonableness:

**Fuel (largest category):**
- Go to Fuel tab
- Note total fuel cost
- Should be 40-60% of total expenses
- Check if any fuel discounts were applied

**Example:** KES 720,000 (52% of total expenses) ✓

**Other Expenses:**
- Go to Others tab
- Review each category:
  - Clerk fees: KES 85,000
  - Milage fees: KES 320,000
  - Parking: KES 45,000
  - Repair costs: KES 175,000
  - Extra costs: KES 35,000
- Total others: KES 660,000

**Verify:** Fuel + Others = Total Expenses
- KES 720,000 + KES 660,000 = KES 1,380,000 ✓

### Spot Unusual Expenses

Look for anomalies:

**Red flags:**
- Repair costs > 15% of total expenses (vehicles deteriorating?)
- Extra costs > 5% of total expenses (poor cost control?)
- Single trips with very high expenses (data entry errors?)
- Expense categories with suspicious patterns

**Example investigation:**

Filter to show only trips with repair costs > KES 20,000:
- KBZ 456C: KES 45,000 (engine repair)
- KBZ 456C: KES 38,000 (transmission issue)
- KBZ 456C: KES 32,000 (electrical repair)

**Finding:** Same vehicle has had KES 115,000 in repairs this month alone. Recommend replacement evaluation.

## Step 4: Dashboard Analysis

Use the Dashboard to get the big picture and identify trends.

### Key Financial Metrics

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Dashboard">
    Navigate to the main Dashboard
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set date filter">
    Filter to your reporting month
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the three summary cards">
    - Total Income: KES 2,450,000 (vs previous period)
    - Total Expenses: KES 1,380,000 (vs previous period)
    - Total Profit: KES 1,070,000 (vs previous period)
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Calculate key ratios:**

**Profit Margin:**
```
(Total Profit ÷ Total Income) × 100
(1,070,000 ÷ 2,450,000) × 100 = 43.7%
```

**Expense Ratio:**
```
(Total Expenses ÷ Total Income) × 100
(1,380,000 ÷ 2,450,000) × 100 = 56.3%
```

**Benchmarks:**
- **Excellent:** > 40% profit margin
- **Good:** 30-40% profit margin
- **Concerning:** < 25% profit margin

### Review Dashboard Charts

**Financial Overview (Bar Chart):**
- Shows income vs expenses trend
- Are you consistently profitable each day?
- Any days with losses?

**Top Destinations (Bar Chart):**
- Which routes generated most revenue?
- Focus on these profitable routes
- Consider raising prices on lower-revenue routes

**Expense Breakdown (Donut Chart):**
- Where is money going?
- Fuel should be largest (40-60%)
- Are other categories growing?

**Trip Completion Trend (Line Chart):**
- How many trips per day?
- Any unusual dips (slow days, breakdowns)?
- Consistent volume or volatile?

**Vehicle Performance (Bars):**
- Which vehicles are most profitable?
- Any unprofitable vehicles?
- Vehicles with no trips (why not)?

### Month-over-Month Comparison

Compare this month to last month:

| Metric | January 2026 | December 2025 | Change |
|--------|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Income | KES 2,450,000 | KES 2,280,000 | +7.5% |
| Expenses | KES 1,380,000 | KES 1,290,000 | +7.0% |
| Profit | KES 1,070,000 | KES 990,000 | +8.1% |
| Trips | 85 trips | 78 trips | +9.0% |

**Insights:**
- ✅ Income growing faster than expenses (good)
- ✅ Profit increasing (excellent)
- ✅ More trips completed (operational efficiency)
- Action: Maintain current performance, investigate what's working

## Step 5: Report Generation

Now create formal reports to share with stakeholders.

<Note>
**🔒 Admin Only** - Only Admin users can generate reports. Staff users can view data but cannot create reports.
</Note>

### Generate Expense Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Expenses → All tab">
    This shows complete expense picture
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set your date filter">
    Filter to the reporting month (Jan 1-31, 2026)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Generate Report">
    Look for the "Generate Report" button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter report name">
    Use clear naming: "January 2026 Fleet Expenses"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create">
    Report is generated and saved to Reports section
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What's included:**
- All expense categories
- Trip-by-trip breakdown
- Category totals
- Overall total expenses
- Date range covered

### Generate Income Reports (if needed)

For client invoicing, you may need rate reports:

**Process varies by client type:**

**1. Single large client (e.g., monthly contract):**
- Go to Incomes → Rate tab
- Filter to client's destination
- Filter to the month
- Generate Rate Report
- Include client name, container details if applicable, VAT

See [Generating Rate Report](/features/reports/generating-rate-report) for details.

**2. Multiple small clients:**
- Generate separate rate report for each major client
- May combine minor clients into "Miscellaneous" category

### Custom Analysis Reports

You might also want to create:

**Vehicle Performance Report:**
- Use Dashboard → Vehicle Performance data
- Screenshot or export
- Shows profitability per vehicle

**Route Profitability Report:**
- Use Dashboard → Top Destinations
- Identify most profitable routes
- Share with sales/operations team

**Fuel Efficiency Report:**
- Go to Expenses → Fuel tab
- Calculate fuel cost per trip
- Compare across vehicles
- Identify inefficient vehicles

## Step 6: Report Distribution

Share reports with the right stakeholders.

### Who Gets What

**Company Owner/CEO:**
- ✅ Dashboard summary (screenshot or PDF)
- ✅ Month-over-month comparison
- ✅ Key insights and recommendations
- ❌ Detailed expense breakdowns (too much detail)

**CFO/Finance Manager:**
- ✅ Complete expense report
- ✅ Income breakdown
- ✅ Profit analysis
- ✅ Budget vs actual comparison
- ✅ Any anomalies or issues found

**Operations Manager:**
- ✅ Vehicle performance data
- ✅ Trip volume trends
- ✅ Fuel efficiency metrics
- ✅ Maintenance/repair costs per vehicle
- ❌ Profit details (unless they need it)

**Clients:**
- ✅ Their specific rate reports
- ✅ Trip summaries for their deliveries
- ✅ Detention fee breakdowns if applicable
- ❌ Your overall company financials

### Report Sharing Methods

**Option 1: Travada Reports Section**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Reports">
    Navigate to Reports section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find your reports">
    All reports you generated are listed here
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Copy link">
    Click "Copy Link" next to each report
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Share via email">
    Paste link in email to stakeholder
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Benefits:**
- Recipients always see current version
- No file size limits
- Can track views

**Limitations:**
- Recipients need Travada access (or public link if enabled)
- Reports expire after 30 days by default

**Option 2: Download and Email**

1. Open the report in Travada
2. Download as PDF or Excel
3. Attach to email
4. Send to stakeholders

**Benefits:**
- Works for anyone (no Travada access needed)
- Permanent record (doesn't expire)
- Can be printed or archived

**Limitations:**
- File attachments can be large
- If data changes, report is outdated

## Month-End Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure you complete all steps:

**Data Verification:**
- [ ] All trips for the month are recorded in Expenditure
- [ ] Trip count matches expected volume
- [ ] Spot-checked 10-20% of trips for accuracy
- [ ] Added any missing trips with correct dates

**Income Review:**
- [ ] Total income verified in Incomes → All tab
- [ ] Rate income subtotal confirmed
- [ ] Detention income subtotal confirmed
- [ ] Major clients reconciled (amounts match expectations)

**Expense Review:**
- [ ] Total expenses verified in Expenses → All tab
- [ ] Fuel expenses checked (40-60% of total?)
- [ ] Other expenses reviewed by category
- [ ] Unusual expenses investigated and documented

**Dashboard Analysis:**
- [ ] Three summary cards reviewed (Income, Expenses, Profit)
- [ ] Profit margin calculated and benchmarked
- [ ] Dashboard charts reviewed for insights
- [ ] Month-over-month comparison completed
- [ ] Key trends and patterns identified

**Report Generation:**
- [ ] Expense report generated and named clearly
- [ ] Client-specific rate reports created (if needed)

- [ ] All reports saved in Reports section

**Report Distribution:**
- [ ] Owner/CEO report prepared and sent
- [ ] Finance team reports shared
- [ ] Operations reports distributed
- [ ] Client invoices generated and sent
- [ ] Reports archived for future reference

## Troubleshooting Month-End Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Numbers don't add up between sections">
    Check your date filters - they must be identical across all sections:
    - Expenditure: Jan 1-31
    - Incomes: Jan 1-31
    - Expenses: Jan 1-31
    - Dashboard: Jan 1-31
    
    Even one day's difference will cause mismatches.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Trip count seems too low">
    Common causes:
    1. Late month trips not yet recorded (check with drivers)
    2. Weekend trips forgotten
    3. Part-time drivers not submitting trip info
    4. Emergency trips not documented
    
    Cross-reference fuel receipts - you should have one receipt per trip minimum.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Profit margin suddenly dropped">
    Investigate:
    1. Did fuel prices increase? (Check recent fuel costs per liter)
    2. Unusual repair costs this month? (Filter Expenses → Others → Repair)
    3. Are you charging clients enough? (Compare rates to expenses)
    4. Data entry errors? (Spot-check high-expense trips)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't generate reports (button missing)">
    You need Admin privileges. If you're Staff, ask an Admin to:
    1. Generate the reports for you, or
    2. Upgrade your role to Admin
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Report expired before I could share it">
    Reports expire after 30 days by default. Options:
    1. Regenerate the report (if still in same month period)
    2. Download reports immediately after generating
    3. Request admin to adjust expiry settings (if available)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Advanced Month-End Analysis

Once you master the basics, add these analyses:

### Year-over-Year Comparison

Compare this January to last January:

- Income growth rate
- Expense control (are costs growing slower than revenue?)
- Profit improvement
- Trip volume changes

### Budget Variance Analysis

If you set monthly budgets:

| Category | Budget | Actual | Variance | % |
|----------|--------|--------|----------|---|
| Income | KES 2,500,000 | KES 2,450,000 | -50,000 | -2% |
| Fuel | KES 700,000 | KES 720,000 | +20,000 | +3% |
| Repairs | KES 120,000 | KES 175,000 | +55,000 | +46% |

**Action:** Repair costs significantly over budget - investigate why.

### Per-Vehicle ROI

Calculate return on investment for each vehicle:

```
Monthly Vehicle ROI = (Vehicle Profit ÷ Vehicle Book Value) × 12
```

Helps decide which vehicles to keep, replace, or add more of.

### Client Profitability Ranking

Rank clients by profit margin:

1. Calculate profit per client (income - costs for their trips)
2. Rank highest to lowest
3. Focus on retaining high-profit clients
4. Renegotiate or drop low-profit clients
